There is a phrase that I used quite a bit last year and I'm about to use it for the first time of the season.  This is a phrase that Cubs fans don't get to use often because it seems we rarely have a team worthy of it.  I haven't heard many baseball fans use it, but I'm sure they do.  

This week, the Cubs play the Brewers and the Cardinals.  The two teams closest to them in the division right now.  I don't think the Cardinals will be there in September, but it's baseball and crazier things have happened.  I do think the Brewers will be in contention throughout the season.  So..."This next week will show us alot about where the team is right now". 

I'm sure you've heard different wordings "This next series/week will be the test" or "This next series/weak will give us a good measurement"...it's all the same. 

The great thing is, I will probably say this at least a dozen times this season.  Whether I'm referring to a game, series or stretch of series, I will undoubtedly judge this team based on those measurements. 

I will also say that "this game" or "this play" will turn the tide of the season one direction or the other.  As fans, it seems that we give momentum such a high value.  I assume professional athletes do not view games in this way.  Let's hope that Soto doesn't believe in momentum and avoids striking out for the 9th time in a row tonight.
