The Cubs entered the series 14-16 and ended the series 15-18


The Reds entered the series 16-15 and ended the series 18-16

Friday, May 6th--Reds 5, Cubs 4

The Cubs continued to set sail on their failboat toward their destination of total offensive ineptitude.  They  left eight on base and were 2-12 with runners in scoring position.  Garza didn't have that great of an outing as he gave up five earned runs.  The biggest blow was Jay Bruce's three-run homer in the fourth.  The good news is Garza got his first big-league hit.  Sweet.

 

Saturday, May 7th--Cubs 3, Reds 2

You're welcome.

This game was nothing to scream about since the Cubs offense was as anemic as ever for most of the game.  It drives me crazy to see Bronson Arroyo shut down the Cubs.  The game was highly entertaining in the ninth inning, however.  The Cubs won the game on a single by Fukudome.  Carlos Pena had tied the game earlier in the inning on a solo homer.  His bat has begun to warm up.  I'm not saying it's blazing hot, but at least it's tepid.


 

Sunday, May 8th--Reds 2, Cubs 0

I actually got to watch this turd.  I figured the Cubs would have a great chance to touch up Cueto with it being his first start of the season.  I thought for sure he would be rusty.  Not the case.  Cueto pitched six innings of shutout baseball.  The Cubs also failed to get to the Reds bullpen, totally wasting Ryan Dempster's quality start.

First Breath After Coma

Carlos Pena seems to be getting on track somewhat at the plate.  He drew three walks on Friday and had a three-hit game on Saturday.  He also hit his first homer at Wrigley.  We will talk about Pena on the next podcast and discuss our thoughts on whether he will continue to deliver for the Cubs.

Uh-oh?

Starlin Castro is mired in his first slump of the season.  He is 7-51 in the last 12 games, and his cold hitting is coming right on the heels of being featured on the cover of Sports Illustrated.  Castro will also be an upcoming podcast topic.

Up Next

The Cardinals come to Chicago for a three-game series.  Though Pujols hasn't been hitting very well, Holliday and Berkman have been tearing it up.  The Cubs pitchers will have to shut these tools down if the Cubs want to beat the Cards.  Theriot also will be playing at Wrigley for the first time since his comment about being on the "right side of the rivalry."  Enjoy the boos and your terrible soul patch.
