When the Cubs sent Tony Campana to Iowa to begin the 2012 season, I stated on that week's episode of The Ivy Envy Podcast that I did not like the move because Campana brings an element to the game that nobody else on the roster can bring. Campana has demonstrated that element in the last two games.

In the 8th inning of Monday's game, the Cubs brought Campana in to pinch-hit and Campana delivered with a one-out single with the game tied 2-2. During Darwin Barney's at-bat, Campana went from 1st to 3rd via two stolen bases. Starlin Castro then stranded Campana at third base to end the 8th inning. The Cubs went on to win the game in the bottom of the 9th, taking game one of the series with the Cardinals.

Campana scored the game-winning run in the 10th inning of Tuesday night's game. Again with one-out, Campana got a base-hit and swiped 2nd base, albeit on a questionable call. Then with two outs, Alfonso Soriano came through with a hit, which was deflected off Cardinals 2nd baseman, Tyler Greene. Campana scored from 2nd and the Cubs won their second straight game in walk-off fashion.

Campana's job is to get on base and get himself in position to score. In his short time with the Cubs since being recalled, Campana is batting .556 with an OBP of .600. Campana has 4 stolen bases in 9 plate appearances. He's getting on base and getting into scoring position.

Since the Cubs are clearly not going to be a power-hitting team in 2012, they are going to need to generate runs and there's nobody close to Campana in the ability to generate runs. As we saw in Monday night's game, it's going to be up to those following Campana to get him in.

While Campana's average and on-base percentage will surely drop, there's no question that he is valuable to this team. It's good to see him in Chicago and helping the Cubs win.
