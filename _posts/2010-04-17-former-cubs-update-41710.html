Matt Murton


A few months ago, I noticed that Matt Murton was playing in Japan. He's playing outfield for the Hanshin Tigers in the Central League of Nippon Professional Baseball.  The Rockies sold Murton’s rights to the Japanese team in December.  Murton would have been arbitration-eligible after batting .324/.389/.499 in Triple A in 2009.  He did spend 29 games with the Rockies last year, but the Rockies outfield was fairly crowded and there just wasn’t space for him in the future.  Murton signed with the Tigers for 2 years and 100 million yen, with a 50 million yen signing bonus.  As best as I can tell, that’s about $1 million USD with a $500,000 signing bonus.  The second year of the contract is a club option, but I don’t see any reason that they wouldn’t pick it up, given his current production.

The Tigers are about three weeks into their season, and I thought this would be a good opportunity to check on Matt's progress.

Through yesterday, the Tigers are 9-8.  Murton is serving as their leadoff hitter and seems to have settled into Japanese baseball.  Let’s look at some of his stats.

Murton is leading the Tigers in many offensive categories (.377/.442/.594)  He is in the top 10 in these categories for the Central League leaders.  Murton is the only Tiger with a stolen base, and he’s stolen four.  He started the season by reaching base in 15 straight games.

Reed Johnson


After earning $3 million in 2009 with the Cubs, the Riverside, CA native Johnson signed a one-year deal with the Dodgers in February for $800,000 and another $250,000 in incentives that are based on games played and plate appearances.

Reed has appeared in 8 of the Dodgers’ 10 games in 2010 as the fourth outfielder.  He’s spent time in the corner outfield positions.  He is batting .333 (6-18) with a double, a triple and an RBI in the young season. Milton Bradley


Oh, where to start with the self proclaimed “Kanye West and Ron Artest of baseball”?  Things did not start well for Bradley this season.  He went 1-22 in the first seven games of the season.  It’s usually not his play on the field that places Bradley in the headlines though, and that has not changed.  He has flipped off fans in Texas, blown kisses to fans in Oakland and shattered a bat after striking out.

Mariners manager Don Wakamatsu had a sit-down with Milton this last Monday and the team decided they needed to take an effort to show Bradley their appreciation for him carrying the team to a 3-0 win on Tuesday and doused him with beer.  Though Bradley is not hitting the cover off the ball, he has had some timely hitting in the last few games and he’s raised his batting average to .147.

We know how Bradley works though.  The highs and lows will surely provide us Cubs fans entertainment this season, especially if Carlos Silva continues to pitch like he has in his first few starts.


Aaron Miles


The Cubs traded Aaron Miles and Jake Fox to the Oakland A’s in December for Jeff Gray, Ronny Morla and Matt Spencer.  Less than a month later, Miles was traded to the Reds in the off-season by the A’s for Willy Tavaras and Adam Rosales.  It didn’t take long for the Reds to identify that Miles is an awful player and he was released on the first day of the 2010 season.
