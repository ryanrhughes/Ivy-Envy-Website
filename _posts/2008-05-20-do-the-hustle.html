Geovany Soto "dashed" around the bases last night like only a catcher can.  Almost 49 years after it last happened, a Cubs catcher hit an inside the park homerun.  Soto is quickly helping me forget   about Michael Barret and Andy forget about Jason Kendall.  Both of whom should definitely be forgotten.  Hopefully Soto will amount to more than another Cubs rookie that wore #18...Dwight Smith.

But as I watched Soto leg this one out, all I could think about was, "What if Ramirez hit that ball".  I guarantee you that he wouldn't have started sprinting to ensure that the hit was a homerun one way or another.  I am fairly certain that Ramirez would have ended up standing on first base...maybe second.  Maybe Ramirez took note of Soto's action.  I hope so.

And I hate to harp on this, but if I see Ramirez hit one more infield distance flyball, I'm going to scream.  I would like to know how many of those he hits in one game.  I bet he hits at least 2 or 3 a game that are either in the infield, in the infield foul territory, or the first five rows of the stands.

Hopefully this All-Star will learn from guys like Theriot, Fontenot, Fukudome and Soto.
