The Cubs were dealt another series loss in Seattle this week, salvaging the last game of the series.  Before Andy and I recorded Podcast 2.15, we were discussing that it feels like the Cubs have had a lot of series in which they lose the first two games and win the third.  So let's take a look at the numbers of the series in the 2010 season, so far.

After the series loss to the Mariners, the Cubs are 7-16-1 in series in 2010.  Though the Cubs have lost a lot in 2010, they've only been swept once (remember the Pirates?).   They have lost 2/3 of the series they've played.

Of the first 25 series (including the first game in this weekend's White Sox series), the Cubs have lost game 1 of the series 17 times.  That is nearly 70% of the series, thus far.

As Andy and I were discussing this, I made the comment that it seems like they lose the first two games of a series and then somewhat salvage it with a win in the third game.  We went through to see how many series ended up L-L-W.  This has happened 9 times this season.  That's about 40% of the series this season, and 75% of the series they have lost 2-1.  Mathematically speaking (which I don't often), you would expect this to happen about 1/3 of the time, so that 75% really stands out and seems odd to me.

Usually, when a team wins the third game of a series to avoid a sweep, the hope is that they carry that momentum into the next series.  Of the 9 times they have won the third game of a series to avoid a sweep, they have won the first game of the next series twice.  That's 22% of the time.

So we've established a few things here.

1.  The Cubs lose a lot of series - way more than they win.

2.  The Cubs often avoid sweeps by winning the final game of a series.

3.  The Cubs don't carry any momentum of that win into the next series.
