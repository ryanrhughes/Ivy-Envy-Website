1.  The Cubs, yet again, make an un-proven and mediocre (at best) pitcher look like the best pitcher in baseball.  I understand that if guys haven't faced a pitcher before, it might take an at-bat to get used to his release.  But Lehr wasn't getting his fastball out of the mid-80's and he held the Cubs to four measley hits.  That's right, a lineup with Derek Lee, Milton Bradley, Aramis Ramirez and Alfonso Soriano could only manage four hits.  And one of them came from Harden.  This dude is 32 years old and just pitched his second career start tonight....a four hit, complete game shutout...against the two-time defending division champions.  Good for him.

2.  Soriano got picked off first base.   Soriano continues to demonstrate that his head is never in the game.  I don't care how many lead-off homeruns he's hit.  I don't care how hot he can get at times.  He's horrendous in the outfield and he's lazy in all aspects of the game.  In a game where it was evident that the team would need to manufacture runs against an un-hittable pitcher in ....Justin Lehr, Soriano got caught with his hands on his hips leading off first base.  But why would he change his game? Lou isn't going to bench him for getting picked off or jogging to a ball rattling around in the corner while the guy that just hit it is gunning for a triple. Plus that monster paycheck is coming in regardless of his performance.

I seriously want to hear a solid pro-Soriano argument.
