This upcoming Cubs vs. Rays series is probably the most interesting series of the season thus far.  And it will hold that title until this weekend when the White Sox stroll into Wrigley.  I wouldn't have imagined in April that this series could be this interesting.  But here we are in mid-June and the Cubs are 20 games over .500 and facing a Rays team that is 2 games out of first place in the mighty American League East and sitting at 11 games over .500.  It's hard enough for me to grasp the fact that the Cubs are 20 games over .500.  And the Rays are a franchise that have only seen .500 ball a handful of times. 

This is a different Rays team.  They have the third best home record at 26-11.  Now I'm not saying the Rays will necessarily be in the thick of things come October, but this is isn't late April.  It's mid-June and I wouldn't put the Rays in the "Fraud" category. 

But Lou built this team to win and they have some GOOD young talent.  So now Lou goes home to Tampa.  He's like the guy that has a slightly dorky girlfriend in high school, but by dating him, she gained some confidence and became pretty popular.  That girl is the Tampa Bay Rays.  She was only a sophomore and he went away to the state university.  She becomes popular and pretty, but then Lou comes back over fall break from college and he brings this gorgeous sorority girl back that he's dating.  That gorgeous sorority girl is the Cubs.  So I expect to see some awkward situations and a little jealousy this week. 

The next two weeks will be really interesting.  Half of the games are against the South Siders who are in first place in the AL Central.  3 games are against the up and comers of the AL East.  And I will be attending one of the games of the Cubs vs. Orioles series. 

Go Cubs.
