Cubs fans learned of another aspect of the change in culture with the organization that will be implemented this year. Theo Epstein plans to reign in players in regards to their night-life. In fact, he went so far as to call the organization's attitude towards the late-night hours up to this point, as a failure.

Epstein attributes this lifestyle as a contributing factor in some of the failed Northside careers and a hinderance to the organization in it's quest for a World Series title. With bars in the Wrigleyville area open until 4am, it's easy to see how that could impact someone playing a day game. Not only does it negatively impact the play on the field, but as we've seen in this last off-season with Starlin Castro, there are other consequences.

With a club that has some talented youth on the team, and a lot more of it to come in the next few years, this is a good time to implement policies like this. In the past, players have ruled the Wrigley roost, but times have changed.

I love this clear organizational shift. The emphasis being put on winning and getting to the point where winning can consistently happen is refreshing and should be encouraging to every Cubs fan. I think we'll hear some criticisms of Ricketts/Epstein this year with a 2012 team that is most likely going to not only lose quite a few games, but also experience some growing pains as the youngsters start making their way up. This is another piece of the foundation of the organization. A foundation that has not existed and needs to be built if the Cubs want to sustain anything.
