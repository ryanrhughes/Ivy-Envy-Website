There aren't too many good things to say about the Cubs right now.  I watched all of Tuesday night's terrible game and only some of last night's equally terrible game.  What the crap is up with Wells?  It's too bad that Marshall and Gorzelanny are both lefties because it's looking like Wells should get a demotion.  The Cubs mostly still seem to have a terrible approach at the plate--swinging for the fences when the situation calls for getting on base (not to mention the fact that they are hitting at pitcher-friendly Safeco).  The double-play ball also seems to be showing up a lot lately too.  Twenty men left on base yesterday, with Lee and Nady each having five a piece.

However, there are a couple players showing up to play.  Though Soto didn't have the best game last night, he didn't leave anyone on base, and he seems to be continuing his patience at the plate.  He drew two walks in Tuesday night's game, and his OBP is .405 currently.  Soriano also continues to hit, and lately has been getting a lot of base hits.  Byrd seems to be working well in the lead off spot--he has four hits in the past two games.  Imagine that!  The Cubs' lead off man getting on base.  Still the middle of the order has been abysmal, so Byrd's efforts have been wasted.  When I listened to Veto's interview with Ramirez, it was nice to hear that Ramirez does seem to realize the urgency of the situation.  Maybe he can come in there tomorrow and put a charge in the lineup.

This team seems to be waving the white flag... and it doesn't have a "W" in the middle of it.
