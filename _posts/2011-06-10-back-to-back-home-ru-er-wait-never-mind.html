If you missed last night's game between the Cubs and the Phillies, you missed one of the most entertaining games of the year (in my stupid opinion).  This game had it all.  It had

  • a Campana head-first slide into first (he was out)
  • a bullet hit straight back at Sean Marshall's head that he caught in self-defense
  • a thrilling, hour-long-plus rain delay
  • an appearance by every position player except for Koyie Hill
  • a shaky John Grabow inning, replete with a balk
  • Fieldin Culbreth as the home plate ump
  • a Carlos Pena collision with the fence that appeared fierce enough to cause yet another injury


But best of all, it had an apparent home run hit by Tyler Colvin called a double after some hot replay action.  Colvin was riding a torrid 0-34 streak at the plate when this craziness occurred.  I was ridiculously excited after what appeared to be back-to-back homers by the Cubs.  That excitement soon turned sour when the homer was ruled a double due to fan interference.  Here is the link to the replay, in case you missed it.  It looked to me like the ball was going to bounce off the top of the fence had the fan not interfered.  Though he reached over the fence, it looked to me like the ball struck him in the forearm.  At any rate, the Cubs ended up winning the game later, so it became a non-factor.  Anyone else out there have an opinion on this play or on instant replay in general?  I'm sure eventually the Cubs will have an instant replay call go their way.
