It's been a long time since I've posted anything. In fact, my last post was July 20. This may hurt me in my yearly performance review with IvyEnvy president and CEO, Corey. In such tough economic times I'm hoping the 2010 budget allows me to stay on staff, but I would understand if Corey's iron fist were to downsize my position. Ad revenues are down and the great state of Illinois has upped the liquor tax making it the highest in the nation. These things don't help my cause.

And so I return to the blog world with the following upbeat and positive message from Debbie Downer and Captain Obvious. They're not going to make it. With a 7-3 record, the past ten games have given a little fuel to the "they're still in it" and "don't give up yet" debates. As a group we at ivyeny have pretty much written off the chances of a comeback. Then, with the loss of our beloved Soriano and roster expansion the Cubs go on a tear, winning 10 of 15 in September and now sit just 6.5 games back of the wildcard leading Rockies. Whoa! 6.5 games...that's doable, right? Not really. They'd have to play even better. The Rockies have continued to play well in September with little sign of a falloff. The Rockies have 15 games remaining. If they finish one game over .500 (not likely to happen) their record will be 91-71. Just to tie, the Cubs will need to finish their 18 games at 16-2. Not an easy task.

This isn't pessimism talking, it's realism. I have to admit I got caught up in the thought that if they continued their winning ways they might make it. Then I did the ridiculous math above and found out my hopes were not founded in reality. I think I need to take a page from Andy's book and just watch the games for the pure pleasure of watching baseball.
