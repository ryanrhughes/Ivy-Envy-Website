Let's talk about the defensive play of the Cubs yesterday.  Most articles I read focus on Dempster's poor performance, but let's not forget the fielding mistakes that cost the Cubs runs.  When the score was still 0-0, Patterson caught a fly ball and very slowly and nonchalantly tossed it back in while one of the Sox took second base.  That runner would eventually score the first run.  Plays like that don't show up in the error column, but they cost the team.  I realize that Patterson

is playing a position with which he isn't very familiar, but he needs to at least hustle.  The defense bears the same burden of placing the pitcher in a position to win that the offense does.  The Cubs are 1-3 in the last four games, and the only errorless game out of those four was the lone win against the Orioles.  Do the Cubs fielders need to run a bunch of little league drills where they "see the ball into the glove?"  Not necessarily.  However, they do have to be intentional about playing focused baseball every day.
