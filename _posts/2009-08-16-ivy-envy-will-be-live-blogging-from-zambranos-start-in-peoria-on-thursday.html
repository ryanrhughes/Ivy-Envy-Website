This Thursday evening, I will get to witness in person, for the first time, Carlos Zambrano pitching.  I am making the trek down to Peoria for Zambrano's rehab start and I'll be live blogging on the site during the game.  Those same blog entries will be sent to our Twitter, so you can follow us on there and get the "groundbreaking news".

I'm really looking forward to this.  I got seats 10 rows behind homeplate, so I will have an up-close view of Zambrano mowing through the Clinton Lumberkings.  I do own a Lumberkings hat, but I won't be sporting it that evening.  I don't wear it often because it has a strong smell of the corn processing and rendering plants in Clinton.

I just wish the Midwest League let the pitchers hit because I can only imagine Zambrano would be swinging for the fences in Peoria to put on a show.  Given Zambrano's track record with success after DL stints, or any injuries at all, I'm looking forward not just to seeing him in Peoria, but getting back to the Cubs.
