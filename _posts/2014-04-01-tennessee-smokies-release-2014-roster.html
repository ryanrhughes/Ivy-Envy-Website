The Tennessee Smokies may be the hottest ticket in minor league baseball in 2014. The Minor League Report is a podcast that covers MiLB and they recently ranked the Iowa Cubs and the Tennessee Smokies in top 5 when they ranked their top teams in MiLB. Iowa was #1 and Tennessee was #5. While Javier Baez plays a huge role in Iowa being ranked #1, I believe Tennessee is potentially a more interesting team than even Iowa.

Tennessee will start with 3 of the top 5 Cubs prospects in the system, according to Baseball America. Kris Bryant (#2 Cubs/#8 overall), CJ Edwards (#3 Cubs/#28 overall) and Jorge Soler (#5 Cubs/#41 overall) will start 2014 in Tennessee. When you also consider that three more prospects in the Cubs top 10 could move up to Tennessee from Daytona (Almora, Candelario and Vogelbach), I think Tennessee is the hottest ticket in MiLB in 2014.
