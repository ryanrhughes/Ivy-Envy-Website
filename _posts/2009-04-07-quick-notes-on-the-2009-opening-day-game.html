I wasn't able to see the majority of last night's game.  First off, my dog got loose and I spent the first 30 minutes trying to lure her home.  Then I went to the airport to pick up the in-laws.  From there we went out to eat, so I was able to see a little at the restaurant.  I DVR'd the game, but haven't had a chance to watch it.  Here are a few notes from what I saw:

*  Soriano's season-starting HR was great.  It also leads to more interesting "lead off" discussion.  Is it better to have a guy up there that can give you a quick 1-0 start and put the other team on their heels or to have a guy with speed and a high OBP?  We know what Lou has decided and it gives buffoons like us fodder for months.

*  It was really nice to see Zambrano come out and pitch a strong game.  The guy is a beast and I love that he wears Cubbie Blue.  It was cool to see the Cubs take care of Oswalt.  He's a great pitcher that has dominated everyone, but somehow the Cubs have managed to do well against him.

*  I couldn't help but laugh when I saw Milton Bradley turn his ankle on first base.  It just seemed like the way the Cubs WOULD start a season.  Big money to a big name....good/great Spring Training...injured in the first game.  I was relieved to see him return to the field in the bottom half of the inning.

*  Tonight - Dempster vs. Rodriguez.  I'll take that matchup.
