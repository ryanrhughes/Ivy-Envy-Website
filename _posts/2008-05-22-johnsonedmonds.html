It looks as though the Cubs will face three lefties this weekend, so Reed Johnson will get the starts in Center Field.  We'll see how he does after sitting on the bench four days in a row.  I somewhat understand this platooning in CF,    but it's not ideal for one of the guys to sit on the bench for a whole series. 

Edmonds got this last series in Houston as the Cubs faced three right-handed pitchers.  Edmonds responded by batting .090 with 1 base hit and 1 walk.  Not exactly the offensive production you want from an outfielder.  And now he'll sit on the bench until at least Monday, maybe longer depending on who the Dodgers have on the mound.  The only positive thing I can mention about Edmonds in this last series is that he only struck out once.  Given his massive upper-cut swing, that's pretty good.  From what I've seen, I don't expect Edmonds to be around long.  You could give me that $250,000 and I would only have one less hit than him. 

Reed Johnson might be a little rusty for this series, but I think we might see a little more production.
