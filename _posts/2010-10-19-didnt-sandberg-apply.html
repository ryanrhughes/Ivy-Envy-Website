Since Ivy Envy is a collaborative effort, you might read a few different opinions here on the site in the next week or so about Mike Quade being named the Cubs manager.



I think many Cubs fans were a little surprised today when Mike Quade was named the manager of the team.  After I heard the news this morning, I texted my wife “Quade is the new manager.”  She responded “Didn’t Sandberg apply?”  I found it pretty humorous, but after I thought about it, she (like most Cubs fans) figured it was a sure-thing that Sandberg would be given the job.


Let me start by saying that I didn’t have a strong opinion (despite our site’s tag-line...Strong Opinions and Marginal Analysis of the Chicago Cubs) on the whole Quade/Sandberg debate.  I honestly don’t know enough about either of them as managers.  Both have a proven track record in the minor league management experience.  Sandberg has been on the fast-track through the Cubs minor league system, working his way through Peoria, Tennessee and Iowa in 4 years.  This last season in Iowa, he was named the PCL Manager of the Year.  Quade has 17 years of minor league managing experience.  He was Manager of the Year twice and won the AAA World Series in 1999.


While management experience in the minor leagues is valuable, I can’t help but think that managing in the minor leagues is different than managing in MLB.  I suppose this is where Quade's 3 years of MLB coaching experience could come into play. I've heard many people speculate that Sandberg needs to get some experience with a big league club before he takes over as a manager.


But honestly, I don't think either Quade or Sandberg will be the reason the Cubs win or lose.  Yes, Quade was "successful" after being named interim manager.  However, he had a full 40-man roster to work with, which means you don't need to worry about wearing out your bullpen.  He took over the Cubs when they were well out of the playoff race and there was very little pressure on the Cubs to win.  I wouldn't go so far as to say that the club had been gutted, but they had already traded away a portion of the big-name talent.  The expectations were low and the Cubs were able to play loose and the role of the spoilers. Personally, I believe that Quade's late-season success says more about Piniella's management than anything.  I backed Piniella throughout the season, but I'm starting to think that Piniella was as big of a problem as anyone on the roster or in the front office.


Quade is the anti-Piniella, and you will find these types of hirings all of the time in sports.  Teams have the tendency to hire the opposite of the manager the person would be replacing.  In football, if you have a coach that is offensive-minded and the team put up a lot of points, but had a bad defense and couldn't stop anyone, you can pretty much guarantee that the next guy will be a defense-minded coach.  Dusty Baker was, for the most part, reserved.  He was also notorious for having "his guys".  I don't need to name them, you know who they were.  So who did they replace him with?  Lou Piniella, a hot-head who would hold players accountable and frankly didn't give a shit what people thought of him.  While Sandberg was a very reserved player, his managerial style is quite the opposite.


So who did the Cubs hire?  The anti-Piniella that, during his stint as the interim manager, was on the field coaching players, something we didn't see Piniella doing.  Quade is a quiet and reserved guy that is comfortable and honest with the media.  A typical Piniella press conference was filled with lots of stuttering and the avoidance of questions.  I have seen a few press conferences by Quade and they're actually interesting!  He is honest.  He answers questions immediately and doesn't work his way around them.  It's almost like you're getting the real story.  It is refreshing.


So I don't know where this leaves Sandberg.  All indications are that he's probably bitter.  And that's fine, he wasn't guaranteed this job.  Quade put in 17 years in the minors and a few years with the Cubs, why would Sandberg feel he's more qualified as a manager?  In all reality, the Cubs are doing Sandberg a favor by not hiring him to manage this mess that Hendry has created.  That would be setting him up for failure.  Fans would expect him to magically right this ship.  The expectations and pressure would be ridiculous.  Sandberg will probably, at some point in the near future, end up managing another team.  A franchise better suited for a first-time manager.  And perhaps we will see Sandberg's return when Quade is finished.  Then again, this is what I thought when the Marlins hired Girardi and look how that's turned out.


I don't know what this means for Sandberg.  Will he be a bench coach?  Will he return to Iowa?  Will he give a one-fingered salute to Jim Hendry and head to Toronto?  I would assume we'll know in the next week.  Mike Quade is the manager of the Cubs and seeing where this team is, I wouldn't say that's a bad thing to have him at the helm.  I am looking forward to someone other than Lou Piniella leading this team for the next three years.
