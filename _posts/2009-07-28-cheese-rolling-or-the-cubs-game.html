After the way the Cubs played during the 1st half of the season, I thought I would have to give up baseball for the summer and switch to competitive cheese rolling.

But the Cubs have been on a tear since the break (9-2), and they are still in the hunt for the cheese.  The guys who were dead weights for the 1st half have finally been contributing more consistently, and the offense as a whole has been better.

Monday:

The offense did struggle a little versus Magic Wandy, but the 1-1 tie set the stage for an exciting 9th, culminating in Font's failure to connect on the squeeze bunt as well as Soriano's winning grand slam in the 13th.  Soriano was saved from his earlier embarrassment of not running to first after he assumed the umpire was going to call his grounder a foul.  Replays showed fairly conclusively that the ball did not make contact with his foot, and Brenly admonished him for not trying to at least "sell it."  He advised Soriano to "hop around a bit."  Maybe he should turn to soccer for acting help.

The worst part about the botched squeeze play for me was the opportunity to hand that tool Valverde the loss.  At one point during the inning, he spit his three-sticks-of-gum wad out onto our grass.  How dare you!  It was pretty awesome seeing Latroy get the boot for swearing at the ump.  I didn't think the pitch in question was an obvious strike--it was borderline.  The entertainment value of this game was high, and things like Hawkins getting thrown out for making a mountain out of a molehill only made it better.

Tuesday:

Yuck.  The game was fun early on, but it quickly went down the crapper.  The high point was Ramirez tying the game with a three-run homer, but everything went to hell for the Cubs after that.  I want to like Heilman.  I really do--especially since the guy's been a Cubs fan since he was a kid, but every time he gets inherited runners, they seem to always come around and score.  I have a feeling that if they would have left Stevens in the game, he would've gotten out of it.  Once again, our favorite asshat came into the game in the ninth, and once again he spit his prodigious wad of gum onto the field after walking Fukudome.  Valverde better hope he never runs into the Menard's Ground Crew in a dark alley.

Oh and Fulchino of the Astros caught a grounder up the middle in his jersey, which I thoroughly enjoyed.
