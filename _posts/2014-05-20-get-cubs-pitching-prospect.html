Saturday night in Kane County, 6’5’’ righty Tyler Skulina, combined with Nathan Dorris on a no-hitter, the 8th in Cougar history.

Skulina was the Cubs 4th round selection in the 2013 draft and was considered a coup for Chicago based on his 2nd round projection and middle-of-the-rotation potential.

The skills that back Skulina’s substantial ceiling were on full display this weekend against the Quad Cities River Bandits (Astros). The big right-hander went 7.1 innings and fanned five while walking two batters. This was the first time Skulina had eclipsed the 6-inning mark as a professional.

With a solid three-pitch mix and college experience, Skulina could be a fast mover in the Cubs system, though he does have work to do as he climbs the ranks to Wrigley. While his 91-94 mph fastball will play at any level, but according to Baseball Prospectus, Skulina needs to continue developing his off-speed pitches as his arm speed is noticeably slower on change-ups than heaters and his slider can get ‘slurvy’.

Eight starts into his 2014 campaign at Low-A, Skulina boasts a 3.19 ERA and 1.08 WHIP, though he has just 32 strikeouts in 42 frames.

Daytona may not be far off for Skulina and he’ll have a friendly face waiting for him when he gets there; his no-hit partner, Nathan Dorris was promoted to High-A on Sunday.
