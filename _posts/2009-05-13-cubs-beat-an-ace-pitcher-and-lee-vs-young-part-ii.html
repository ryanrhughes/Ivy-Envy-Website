It was really nice to see a bruised and battered Cubs team win last night's game in a manner that you're supposed to when facing an ace pitcher.

1. Count on your pitcher to keep things close. Harden pitched a good game.

2. Attempt to force a high pitch count to push the starting pitcher out early. Peavy had 111 pitches in six innings.

3. Get timely hits and take advantage of bad pitches. Milton Bradley's 2 run homerun.

4. Beat up on the bullpen once you force the starter out. The Cubs added three runs in the last two innings to pad their lead. By the way, I know Andy has already dished out his appreciation, but let's hear it for Bobby Scales.

Tonight's game should be interested. What I would like to see is Derek Lee do more than his 1 walk, 3 strikeout performance last night. I'm not really being critical of Lee. He's proven that he's willing to play through injury and if there was a time that this club needs a veteran to play through injury, it's right now. Maybe facing Chris Young tonight will give Lee that shot in the arm he needs. If you don't recall, they have a bit of a history.
