I've had to endure two days of Cubs fans freaking out over the idea of Bob Howry wearing a Cubs uniform again.  I collected all my thoughts and sat down to write a post.  I wanted to talk about how Bob Howry isn't as bad as we remember.  I wanted to talk about how horrible our bullpen is and how there aren't a lot of options right now.  Then I clicked on my Google Reader and read this post on AnotherCubsBlog.net. 
Spot Freaking On.

In the last few days, I've posted some on our Twitter account in favor of the move.  When it comes to relief pitchers, we remember the worst and forget about the rest.  The blown late innings games stand out the most. I remember 2008 Bob Howry.  Up to this point, 2010 Bob Howry has been even worse than 2008 Bob Howry.  But I don't know what goes on behind the scenes.  Perhaps Bobby needs a new situation.  The Arizona bullpen (even worse than ours) is enough to make anyone desire some new scenery.

So let's look past 2008 Bob Howry.  I know it's a difficult thing to do. But, 2006 and 2007 Bob Howry were pretty efficient out of the bullpen. His ERA was 3.17 and 3.32, respectively. He also had a nearly 4-1 strikeout to walk ratio. After the horrendous 2009 season that gives us chills, Howry had a decent season last year with the Giants. He didn't appear in as many games, but got his ERA back down to 3.39. He walked a few more guys and his K to BB ratio was at 2-1. Now, 2010 Bob Howry has just been horrendous. He's got an ERA over 10 and no redeeming statistic that I could find in my 18 seconds of research.

But relief pitchers are streaky. What do the Cubs have to lose? If he sucks, they cut him loose. There aren't many options out there this early in the season. Maybe he'll settle in and go all 2010 Carlos Silva on our asses. I'd rather the Cubs buy some disposable veteran that has had a decent career rather than pull up some youngsters from the farm system and ruin them by throwing them to the wolves. Put it on the record, I am in favor of the Bob Howry experiment.
