There's been quite a bit of discussion in the Cubs blog-world about benching slumping veterans in favor of the young hot hands.  I don't understand this and I don't mean to discount Hoffpauir's talents, but I'd take Derek Lee in the lineup over Hoffpauir every game.

This is typically one of those arguments that is purely speculation and there's really not much of a way to prove or disprove either side.  However, I think that last night's 2-1 loss to the Dodgers was as close as I will get to being able to back my side of the issue.

The point in the game where veteran leadership comes in to play is the late-innings.  That's when "clutch play" is required.  In last night's game, two veterans that have been lambasted for their poor play came through in the 9th inning with clutch hits.  Milton Bradley and Derek Lee started off the 9th inning with base hits and Geovany Soto was intentionally walked.  Then, both Bobby Scales and Jake Fox struck out.  These are unproven guys, but I have heard both labeled as "the hot hand".

I'm not about to fault them for their strikeouts.  It just shows that you can have a lineup full of young talented hot-hands, but proven veterans can handle pressure and are much more likely to come through when you need them.

And yes, I'm aware that Bobby Scales hit a homerun in the 8th.
