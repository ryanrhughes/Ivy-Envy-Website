We've joked about Tony Campana's big floppy glove several times on the podcast, but I also think what he has meant to this team needs to be mentioned.  Campana is the fastest player the Cubs have had in a long time.  He is perhaps one of the fastest players in the majors right now.  Though Aramis Ramirez got the walk-off single in Wednesday night's ballgame against the Giants, it was Campana's speed on the basepaths that put Ramirez in a position to get that hit.  Just having him on base is such a distraction to an opposing pitcher.  He has stolen eight bases in nine attempts, and he has only appeared in 35 games.  This is a revelation for a team whose best base stealer has been Ryan Theriot in the past couple seasons.

Campana's only problem has been getting on base enough.  He is currently hitting .268 with a slightly alarming .307 OBP (Soriano's is slightly higher!).  If he can improve in his hitting and continue to perfect his bunting, he could be an invaluable weapon for the Cubs going forward.

The main thing I wanted to mention is Bob Brenly's quotation about Campana today.  John Cusack was in the booth and asked Brenly if he could ride along in the plane's cargo hold for the Cubs' upcoming trip to Washington.  Brenly responded, "Maybe you could ride in Tony Campana's glove."
