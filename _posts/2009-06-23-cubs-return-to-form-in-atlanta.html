The Cubs jumped down to Atlanta for one game and were shutout for the sixth time this season.  Four of those have come in the last 30 games.  Last night, the Cubs had 10 hits with 12 total baserunners when you include Fukudome's two walks.  And once again, another good pitching performance is wasted.

I was afraid the sweep against the Indians was just a fluke.  The Indians are terrible and their pitching is atrocious.  And even in that series, 2 of the games went into extra innings.  I don't think the Cubs have looked solid since the series where they swept the Padres at Wrigley in the middle of May.  The bats came alive against the Indians, but with that pitching up there in Cleveland, it was hard to get too excited.  The Cubs had to count on Kerry Wood for those two victories.

So I think these next two weeks are pretty important and they will give us a good idea of where this team stands.  The next two weeks include the Tigers, White Sox, Pirates and Brewers.  I think if the Cubs do any worse than 8-5, they may be in trouble.
