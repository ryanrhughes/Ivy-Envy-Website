Thirty-three games left.  Can the Cubs win 100 games for the first time since '35?  I think it is well within the realm of possibility.  We still aren't out of the woods yet as far as the NL Central race is concerned, but every day the promised land gets closer and closer.  In my opinion, the Cubs are almost a lock to win the pennant.  It's when you start to look at how the Cubs match up with potential World Series foes that things get interesting.  But this team is certainly capable of defeating all foes, Ivan Drago style.  "I defeat all men!"

Today's win was fun/encouraging to watch for several reasons.  Harden owned the gnats.  The first at bat of the game set the tone--He started off with pitches in the high eighties and proceeded to touch the nineties.  Harden then notched his first strikeout with an 84 mph change-up that faded off the corner of the plate. 

In a pinch-hit appearance, Fukudome hit a two-run homer.  It was funny how Len and Bob kept talking about how Gerald Perry was trying to emphasize the need for Fukudome to forget about homers and just focus on making good contact.  Well, he made pretty good contact today, and hopefully this indicates a moribund slump.

DeRosa is tearing it up.  Today's solo shot marked his fourth straight game with a homer, and now with every subsequent homer he will set a new single season P.R.

This is the team and the year.
