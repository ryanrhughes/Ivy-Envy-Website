Last weekend, I wrote a post about the distance rule for tickets given away by the Cubs. According to the official rules (at the time), if you reside more than 100 miles from Wrigley Field, you are not eligible to win free tickets.

(Click to enlarge)

The Cubs have informed me that they’ve changed this rule. It was originally a rule that was handed down by Major League Baseball and the Cubs have received an exemption and the rule now reads:

(Click to enlarge)

This is a great move by the Cubs. The number of Cubs fans outside of the 100 mile radius is enormous. The Cubs have shown their loyalty those of us that live a distance away. It would have been easy for the organization to hear these complaints and dismiss them. However, they took the time to address the situation and make the change.
