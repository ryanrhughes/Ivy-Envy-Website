We are above .500 and are only two games behind the first-place Cardinals.  Fans can still be optimistic about how the team will do within this division, but it has become increasingly difficult to hope for much success beyond that.  This series against the Brewers and the upcoming series against the Cardinals will help make the final destination of this team clearer.

Sam Fuld has shown us in his two starts what damage the Cubs can do when their leadoff man gets on base.  Fuld was on base for both of Lee's homers in Thursday's game, but Soriano probably wouldn't have been.  In today's game, Soriano went 0-5, leaving 3 men on base.  That performance doesn't effect success, and it seems that Lou is finally going to move him down in the lineup.  It is nice to see Lee finally hitting something other than solo-shots, and it all begins with the leadoff man finding a way to get on base.

Also, not lost on us was the incredible defensive game the Cubs had on Thursday.  Fuld led the way defensively, but Soto, Fukudome, and Blanco all made plays of the top 10 variety.  If you didn't see this game, you owe it to yourself to check out the highlights here.  What the highlight video doesn't show on Fuld's put-out at the plate is that he hurls his entire body into that throw and flips over himself.  Oh, and Lou was ejected in this game as well.  Overall, the Cubs have been highly entertaining to watch lately.

Which player will the Cubs send down once Ramirez gets back next week?  This is the tough decision the Cubs face.  It will probably be Fuld or Blanco I expect.  I wish the Cubs would turn Miles loose.
