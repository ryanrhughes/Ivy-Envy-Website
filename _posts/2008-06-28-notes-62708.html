Yes, I was at Wrigley for the awful game on Thursday against the Baltimore Orioles.  This game was painful for me to sit through.  I am willing to take responsibility for this ugly game.  I took my brother and dad, both of whom are White Sox fans.  My dad even wore a Cubs hat to attempt to embrace that Cubbie spirit, but I'm guessing him wearing the hat was some sort of hex that Sox fans use against the Cubs.  I hope to find the time in the next few days to share some pictures from Thursdays game with you.

* What has stood out to me the most in the last two days (losses to Orioles and White Sox) is the bad defense.  I was surprised with Edmonds' brain-fart in Center on Thursday, allowing a runner to go from first to third on a routine base hit.  Theriot's error in the fifth inning on Thursdays game was pretty frustrating too.  It didn't lead to any scoring, but it could have been disastrous.  The grounder he booted was off the bat of pitcher Radhames Liz, bringing up the top of the order rather than ending the inning.

* Marquis really looked bad.  The five runs with 2 outs in the 3rd inning took all the life out of Wrigley Field.  Call me crazy, but after the last week, I don't feel quite so optimistic with a large deficit.  Things can't be going well for a pitcher when he gives up 3 doubles in one inning.  He ended up giving up 7 runs and 3 walks in 4 innings. 

* Wrigley also lost some buzz and hope for a comeback when Lee was pulled after he hit into a double play  in the 5th.  Aramis Ramirez was pulled at the same time.  By the way, Derek Lee hit into 3 double plays in a row, one on Thursday and two today.  I wasn't really in the mood for back-ups during the only game I will probably make it to this year.  Daryle Ward and Henry Blanco both came in and knocked runs in.  I was also real happy to see Matt Murton get some playing time when Lou shifted Patterson to Center to replace Edmonds. 

* I caught a quick clip from the post game press conference with Lou Piniella today and he made it quite clear that Eric Patterson will not be in left field tomorrow.  His defense was so poor today (lazy getting the ball in after a popout leading to a tagging runner and an error on a ground ball), it would not surprise me to see him get sent down to Des Moines.  I am not a fan of Eric Patterson and I don't see what he really has to offer the Cubs. 

* All in all, the Cubs are playing poorly in the field.  They seem to be channeling the defense we saw under Dusty Baker.  Bad throws from the outfield, not hitting cut-off guys and lazy outfield play can really kill a hot team.  I'm not ready to throw out the "June Swoon" label yet, but this team looks different than it did a week ago.  I see this team picking up momentum as some of these guys like Zambrano, Johnson and Soriano start returning from injuries.  Unfortunately, the Cardinals don't seem to be fading away like we would like, and I think the Brewers will probably continue their upward movement.  I trust that Lou will keep these guys motivated and improve their recent play.
