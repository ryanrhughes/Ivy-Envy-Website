Well, it took 14 innings, but the Cubs came out on top.  And I had my doubts; I'm not going to lie.  Once it was Patton vs. Cordero, I thought it would be the end for the Cubs.  However, once I saw Mike Lincoln in the 'pen, I knew the Cubs had the whipping boy they were looking for.  A friend of Ivy Envy, James (not Jimmy), made me aware of a quotation from Bob Brenly I had missed today: "Not much to smile about today... unless you're a fan of pitching and bad at-bats."  That about sums it up.  This has been a tough past week for the Cubs.  Even though they have won three out of the last five, four of those games have gone to extra innings.

The team was fortunate to win today's 14-inning marathon.  It would have been a demoralizing loss for the team, and today the bullpen made the win possible.  With tomorrow being a travel day for the team, there is not much rest in the team's future.  However, the Cubs should be able to soundly beat the Astros in their three-game series this week, and that will hopefully boost their morale for the interleague matchup against the Twins this weekend.  I will be attending Sunday's game, and I hope to see a Cubs victory--the Twins could be a tough matchup.

One other thing I wanted to mention--the people clamoring for Hoffpauir to take Lee's spot in the lineup until Lee "can start hitting" don't have much of an argument any more.  Though he has struggled early in the season with RISP, he has been more clutch lately.  Today he came up with a big base-hit with the bases loaded.  It feels as though he is getting to where we need him to be.  In May, Lee hit .313, and in June, he is hitting .348 so far.  To me, the bullpen has becoming a little less of a concern and is being replaced by runners left on base.  The Cubs have got to drive in those runs.  The pitchers shouldering too much of the burden for the team.  Time for the offense to pick them up.
