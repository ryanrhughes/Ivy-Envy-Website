I'll admit that six weeks ago, I could not wait for Spring Training to begin.  I can't stand NBA basketball and just don't follow the NFL or College Basketball closely.  So when College Football ends, I'm ready for Spring Training.  Throughout January and February, all I can think about is baseball starting up.

So now, about six weeks into Spring Training, I'm sick of it.  I'm tired of spotty media coverage.  I'm tired of stories that are only reported on because it's March and there's nothing else to report.  It's not that I don't enjoy position battles.  I just don't like it when those battles are all there is to talk about.  I want to feel the excitement of a close ballgame, a clutch hit or a winning streak.  I want to feel the frustration of poor fielding, cold bats and a losing streak.  That's baseball, and I'm ready.

There's only about a week and a half of Spring Training left.  Then we can talk about things that really matter.  We can talk about topics that are fluid.  We can live and die with our Cubs on a daily basis.

That said, I'm going to go watch the Cubs play the Giants in a Spring Training game on the MLB Network.
