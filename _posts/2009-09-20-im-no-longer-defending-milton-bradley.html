Ok, so I'm a little late to this party.  I've defended him for the entire season for two reasons.

1.  Milton Bradley has abilities.  He's a good baseball player and he could contribute to the Cubs.

2.  Fans are idiots and they were heckling him during Spring Training.  We're talking exhibition while he was playing well.

The whole season, I was just thinking that if the fans could get off his back, Bradley would perform and feel at home.  That's of course ignoring the elephant in the room.  Bradley is a head-case and has, as far as I know, played in every city with a chip on his shoulder.  This is no longer the "loveable losers".  The days of "Yeah, they aren't that good and they have flaws, but gosh darnit, they're our guys and I'll stand by them" are over.

I can't defend him any longer.  I can't defend a guy that criticizes an organization and fans that gave him a $30 million contract for being negative.  This is not a principled stand against some evil corporation.  This is a game.  I can't defend a guy that claims this negativity is why the Cubs haven't won in 100 years.  There are lots of fans of teams that are tough on their players.  New York and Philadelphia come to mind.  Last time I checked, they hadn't gone 100 years without winning.  Maybe Tim could give us a Yankees fan's perspective.

Bradley is wasting years of potential by being a general asshat.  I'm done defending him.  He crossed the line.  There are rules, this isn't 'Nam.  The Milton Bradley experiment has been a miserable failure and I would imagine we will eat a lot of that contract.  And, we have mud on our faces.  Either we're the idiots for being hard on a guy that doesn't have terrible numbers, or we're idiots for taking a chance with him.

This really is the perfect ending for this season.  It's a fine example of the whole season.  What a disaster!  It sure feels like Hendry did more than "tinker" with last year's team, huh?
