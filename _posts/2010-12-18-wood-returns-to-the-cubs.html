Kerry Wood is back!  He returns to Chicago with a one-year, 1.5 million dollar contract.  Maybe having Wood again will restore some virility to the Cubs' pitching staff.  This is a great deal for Chicago.  Apparently, he gave the Cubs quite a discount.  It was reported that Wood was offered a 3.5 mil contract from the White Sox and even more from the Yankees and Red Sox.  As if we needed more reasons to love this guy.  We know how much he loves Chicago, the Cubs, and Cubs fans.  When he became a free agent in 2008, he took out a full-page ad thanking the organization and the fans.  He has also provided some electrifying moments, the most memorable being his 20-K performance in 1998.

But this move isn't just good for nostalgia.  As a member of the Yankees toward the tail-end of 2010, Wood posted a 0.69 ERA, only giving up two earned runs in twenty-six innings pitched.  He is a pitcher who gets a lot of strikeouts, and he will be a great veteran presence in the bullpen.  The only concern is that the Cubs now have two pitchers who tend to walk a lot of batters potentially pitching back-to-back; however, this tandem could secure a victory in a hurry as well.  I'm a fan of this move.
