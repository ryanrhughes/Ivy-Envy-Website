Wednesday night's Peoria Chiefs game was special for a few reasons.  Not only did the Chiefs continue their domination of Clinton and improve their season record to 4-0 against the Lumberkings, but they did it in style.  Cam Greathouse carried a no-hitter into the 6th inning before giving up a single to Tim Morris.  That single ended up being the only Lumberkings hit on the evening.  Greathouse pitched the longest outing for the Chiefs this year, going 7 innings and giving up no runs on just the one hit.  Greathouse walked three and struck out eight.  Yohan Gonzalez came in to pitch the final two innings and he did not give up a hit.

Not only did the Chiefs throw a no-hiter, but they also recorded a triple-play in the game.  In the 7th inning, shortstop Elliot Soto fielded a sharp line drive, flipped the ball to Pierre LePage, who threw to Richard Jones at 1st base for a 6-4-3 triple play.  The Chiefs won the game 3-0.  They not only played well defensively in the game, but every Chief in the starting line-up, except for Arismendy Alcantara got a base hit.
