In W.P. Kinsella's "The Thrill of the Grass," the narrator describes a fellow fan:

He is a fan.  Really a fan.  When the weather is intemperate, or the game not close, the people around us disappear like flowers closing at sunset, but we are always there until the last pitch.  I know he is a man who attends because of the beauty and mystery of the game, a man who can sit during the last of the ninth with the game decided innings ago, and draw joy from watching the first baseman adjust the angle of his glove as the pitcher goes into his windup.


In the past, Cubs fans in general have had a reputation as  being these types of fans.  Is that the case anymore?  It feels like winning is becoming everything.  Can we still just simply sit "with the game decided innings ago" and just enjoy the beauty of the game?  With the Cubs needing a miracle to make it into the playoffs, just enjoying the game is pretty much the only option now.  And that's why many have quit "wasting their time" on the Cubs, moving on to the Bears or college football or whatever.  Yesterday, however, Mike Fontenot reminded us of the beauty of athletic excellence with his sliding catch in foul territory. 

The article uses this play as proof that the Cubs aren't giving up.  I don't know how true that is, but it does prove that excellent plays can happen anytime.  I hope to see plenty of them yet this fall, even with "the game decided innings ago."
