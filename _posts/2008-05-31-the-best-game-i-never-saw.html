I sat at work today, occasionally looking at the score of today's Cubs vs. Rockies game.  After a 4 run deficit in the 1st inning and a 8 run deficit by the middle of the 4th inning, I had all but written off this game.  At least we can take care of them tomorrow and salvage the series...right?  Well, by the 7th inning, I had given up on checking on the game on MLB.com and turned on my radio that doesn't get very good reception in my office.  But a fuzzy Ron Santo is better than tardy update from MLB.com. 

I enjoyed tracking this comeback with my co-worker that is a Cardinals fan.  Edmonds playing a huge role in this comeback made it more entertaining for me.  So, I think this is the game that will be referenced throughout the year.  Not so much in that it will be "the game where the season turned around", but rather an example of this team's ability to put up a lot of runs and their ability to come back from behind.  We have seen throughout April and May that this is a team that you can't count out at any point in the game. 

So I've deemed this game "The Best Game I Never Saw".  It is a game that would have been a blast to watch on tv.  Maybe Comcast will show a rerun tonight or tomorrow and it will become "The Best Game I Never Saw Live".

Two side-comments:

*Can we just play all our games at home?

*Lilly's poor performance can't be lost in the mix of this great win.  Our starting pitching still scares me.
