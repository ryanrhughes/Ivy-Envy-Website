With a month left of the 2009 season, I'm ready to put it to bed.  I realized during this week's podcast that there isn't really much to talk about and there's still 27 games to play.  What more is there to say?  At this point, all conversation about 2009 dies and speculation of what will happen or what needs to happen for 2010 takes over.  But it feels inappropriate to talk about next year when there is still a month of baseball left.  I'll do it anyway.

I read that Chone Figgins might be a good fit for the Cubs.  It's not a secret that the Cubs are lacking a lead-off hitter and overall speed.  Figgins would also be the solution for the Cubs at 2nd base.  The Angels have used him at 3rd base most of the time, but he has played all over the field.  Sound like a former second baseman we lost in this last offseason?

Chone is having a great season and was an All-Star this year for the first time in his career.  But as we learned with Milton Bradley, it's not fair to judge a player based on their one career year and then expect them to meet or exceed that production.  So let's ignore his stats this year and just focus on his career average.  These stats are based on 162 games.

BA: .292

OBP: .363

HR:  5

RBI:  59

SB:  49

CS: 17

This year, his OBP is .400 and it's the second season in which he's flirted with .400.  He has a stolen base success rate of around 75%.  I think 2nd base and the lead-off spot are two fairly big issues that the Cubs need to address in the upcoming off-season.  In the article about this potential move, Piniella points out that the Cubs are last in stolen bases.  This is a very slow team, top to bottom.  Soriano was signed as a speedster, but he's been reduced to a steady trot.  The leader for the team in stolen bases this year is Ryan Theriot, with 16.  Theriot is not fast and it seems like he really has to pick his pitches to steal.  As of today, Figgins has 39 steals for the season.

I don't think Figgins would solve all of the problems that this team has, but it would be an improvement in the leadoff spot.  This team does not have a leadoff hitter and Piniella indicates that if the Cubs don't find one in the off-season, Fukudome will be the lead-off man.  Though I feel that Fukudome filled the role fairly well, he wasn't able to do what a natural leadoff hitter does.   I would welcome Figgins with open arms, even if Baseball-Reference.com tells me that the the most similar hitter to him is Bip Roberts, the man whose baseball card you got in every pack you bought in 1989.
