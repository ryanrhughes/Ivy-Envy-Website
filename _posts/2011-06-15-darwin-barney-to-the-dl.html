A lot of Cubs fans have poo-poo'd the notion of the youth movement, but in the last two days, the youth has pulled through with late-inning heroics.  In last night's game, Tony Campana doubled down the left-field line, advanced to 3rd on a sacrifice bunt by Fukudome and then scored on a Starlin Castro base-hit.

If you didn't see this game, I don't believe anyone else on the team could have stretched the hit into a double.  To give you an idea of how "deep" the hit went, Ryan Braun had to contend with the bullpen pitcher's mound.  This is precisely why I believe the Cubs need to keep Tony Campana up with the team.  He brings a dimension to the team that we literally have not seen in years.  This victory was purely generated by his speed.  If he stays up, Tyler Colvin should stay in Iowa the rest of the year, unless he's batting .450 for the I-Cubs.

Don't get too excited about two wins in a row over the the first-place team in the division.  One of the few bright spots on this 2011 team, Darwin Barney, is headed to the Disabled List after injuring his knee while scoring the game-winning run Monday night against the Brewers.  Barney has quickly become my favorite player on the team to watch and now he'll be gone for two weeks.

Barney entered Spring Training as a player on the periphery.  He worked hard and played well in Spring Training, earning a spot on the team.  Just a few weeks later, he was rewarded with the starting job at 2nd base.  He's been one of the most consistent and reliable players on the team.  He's played in 63 games and has a .294 batting average.  He's within one RBI and two runs scored of leading the team in those statistical categories.  That runs scored number would probably be significantly higher if the heart of the batting order was able to drive in runs.

The good news is that Barney isn't seriously injured.  It is a Grade 1 PCL strain in his right knee.  Barney stated that he could be back within a week.  However, this season is a loss, so they might as well let him recover fully, rather than have him deal with a nagging injury all year by trying to get him back as soon as possible.

Barney's injury will lead to more playing time for Jeff Baker and Blake Dewitt.  Baker is returning from his time on the DL and has played really well this year.  Dewitt has also been playing pretty well.  If Jim Hendry isn't too scared to make some moves, this might be a good opportunity to showcase these guys and trade them while they're playing well.
