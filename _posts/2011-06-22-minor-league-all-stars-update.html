Just like El Guapo had a plethora of piñatas, the Cubs had a plethora of prospects playing in various All-Star Games over the past few days.  A few of us took in the Midwest League All-Star Game here at Modern Woodmen Park in the Quad Cities, so I'll start with an update on how the Peoria Chiefs players performed in the game.  Corey wanted to write about their exploits, but he was too busy watching David "No Way!" Burlet the whole time to know what happened in the game.

Corey particularly enjoyed Burlet's theatrical skipping.

Anyhow, the Peoria Chiefs had two players start the game--Greg Rohan (Ro-hahn, Ro-hann, or Ro-hun?) at third and Matt Szczur in right.  Neither of these guys were able to do anything at the plate, but several Chiefs players were effective coming off the bench.  Austin Kirk went untouched in his 0.2 inning pitched.  Eliot Soto fielded his position at short quite well, despite his Campana-esque stature, and he drove in two runs with a single in the eighth.  Micah Gibbs also singled and scored in the game.  The Chiefs' West Division Team ended up winning 8-3.

We had a great time at the game, and you should come to a game at Modern Woodmen Park if you live anywhere near the Quad Cities.

The Daytona Cubs had six players representing them in the FSL All-Star Game.  Justin Bour (DH) went 1-4 with a double, but Evan Crawford (CF) had a rough night at the plate, with five LOB.  It appears that Junior Lake didn't get into the action, I believe since he had been called up to Tennessee.  Michael Brenly had a big game, collecting two hits and an RBI.  Daytona's pitchers didn't fare too well--Aaron Kurcz gave up three hits, and one of them was a home run.  He was charged with two earned runs in his one inning of work.  Frank Batista also struggled a bit, giving up two hits and one earned run.  Even though Daytona's pitching gave up all three runs scored by the South, the North All-Stars won 5-3.

The Tennessee Smokies had a boatload of players in Southern League's All-Star Game.  They had a team-record twelve players selected--Steve Clevenger, Ryan Flaherty, Marwin Gonzalez, Brett Jackson, Blake Lalli, DJ LeMahieu, Rebel Ridling, Matt Spencer, Marco Carillo, Rafael Dolis, Trey McNutt, and Rob Whitenack.  Not all of these players appeared in the game due to injuries and call-ups, but that is an amazing number of players to be selected.  In the game, Blake Lalli hit an RBI double, and Matt Spencer also collected a hit.  Steve Clevenger was hit by a pitch and would later score.  Rafael Dolis only faced two batters but did not give up a hit.  The North Division won, 6-3.

The Pacific Coast League All-Star Game doesn't take place until July.

Corey and I were discussing one aspect of the Cubs farm system that is very encouraging, and that is the play of the catchers.  Corey mentioned that Wellington Castillo has been playing unbelievably well at Iowa--he earned player of the week honors by batting .625 with 12 RBI over four games.  Gibbs, Brenly, and Clevenger have represented their teams well this season and all seem to be promising players.  I'm a big Geovany Soto fan, but it's nice to know that the Cubs will have plenty of options if he can't produce as expected at the plate.
