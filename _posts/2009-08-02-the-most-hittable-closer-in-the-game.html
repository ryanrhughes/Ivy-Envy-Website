So, Kevin Gregg had back-to-back blown saves this weekend, and tonight's game featured back-to-back homeruns leading to that second blown save.  While Saturday's blown save eventually led to much excitement, today's failure just led to a lot of swearing.  Some Cubs fans who weren't quite ready to kick Gregg to the curb may be ready to do so now.  Corey sent me a text after the game tonight that read, "[Gregg is] the most hittable closer in the game."  Our closer now has a 4.17 era and 5 blown saves on the year.  Last season with the Marlins, Gregg blew 9 total saves.  I figure if he remains the closer, he is on pace to fail several more times than last season.  We probably have at least 4 or 5 more to look forward to as Piniella mentioned after the game that he doesn't plan on making a change at closer.  In the post-game press conference, Piniella sang his old redundant song, "What are you going to do?"  Well Cubs fans have plenty of ideas for what Lou can do.  Here is an interesting one suggesting Harden as closer by Chaim Witz of TMS (this was even posted before this weekend's debacles).  The follow-up post by Ginger Russ is also interesting to consider.  Some people might suggest Marmol, but given his own struggles with command this season, he seems doomed to remain a set-up man for the foreseeable future.  So, what do you think?  Try Guzman?  Put B.J. Ryan in there as soon as possible?  I don't know.
