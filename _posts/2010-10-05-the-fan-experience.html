Now that the season is officially over and we know that there are only seven teams in all of baseball worse than the Cubs, it's time to move on to complaining about other things we can't control. On our last podcast Paul mentioned that he thinks the Cubs need to get rid of Wrigley field. Paul said later that he expected to be attacked for such a statement and was surprised by the lack of reaction from the rest of us.

I'll admit that I've drank the Wrigley kool-aid for a long time. It has a sense of history and charm that you don't find in other stadiums and that's hard to replicate. If you blow it up, you can't get it back. However, after my experience at the September 25 game against the Cardinals, I'm starting to dislike the taste of kool-aid.

Everyone knows there are a lot of really bad seats at Wrigley. I happened to get lucky enough to get two of them. It was the usual, pole in the face and a busy aisle in front of you seats. If we would have stayed in those seats we may have seen about two innings of the game, and not consecutively. We, of course moved to better seats. My problem is in the fact that after the 40% in added fees the tickets cost over $30 each. x2 + a couple beers and we dropped over $80. You're welcome, Cubs. It's frustrating to me that such seats even exist. Just remove them...or sell them for what they're worth, about $5. When I bought the tickets the day they went on sale (expecting a late season race between the Cubs/Cards) it was not disclosed that these seats sucked. If I bought a car that didn't drive I would expect my money back. If I buy a ticket to see a baseball game and the game can't be seen, should I get my money back? I know that's not going to happen, but seriously.

Add to that the junk, including a discarded Toyota sign, that just sits around the place and you've got a sub-standard experience for the highest average ticket price baseball. The Cubs organization has a history of making decisions based purely on how it affects revenue. My hope is that Tom Ricketts will change the ways of the past and start to make decisions on what is best for the fans.

We Cubs fans are loyal. If you change Wrigley it will piss off a lot of people. I think a fan base that can tolerate this much losing can tolerate a little bit of change. Keep what works and get rid of what doesn't.
