Ryan Dempster was placed on the Disabled List with a strained muscle and Randy Wells was recalled from Iowa to fill in for Dempster. I was surprised when Wells was sent to Iowa, but had a hunch it wouldn't be long until the Cubs had an injured starter, paving the way for his return.

How did Wells do in his short time with Iowa? The good news - he had a 1-0 record. The bad news? In the three games he started for the I-Cubs, he had a 9.42 ERA. In his 14 innings pitched, he gave up 15 runs, all of them earned. His last start was his worst performance, a 2.1 inning outing against Round Rock in which he gave up 8 runs on 8 hits.

But Wells is not worried. Although have you really ever heard an athlete admit they are worried? "I feel real good. The ball’s coming off my hand good. I know the ERA doesn’t quite show it, but I’ve worked out some stuff."

It would have been nice if Wells went down to Iowa and dominated AAA during his time there, but the Cubs need a starter and he's the best they have in the reserve tank. We had hoped that Iowa would be a little stronger with pitching this year, especially starting pitching, but that's not really the case. Check out their pitching stats here.

Wells will have at least a few starts to redeem himself and try make the case for him to be kept on the Cubs and one of the struggling back-end starting pitchers to get the I-80 trip back to Des Moines.
