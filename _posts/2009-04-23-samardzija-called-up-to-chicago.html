Jeff Samardzija will pass through my hometown as he heads on I-80 from Des Moines to Wrigley Field.  As a huge Notre Dame fan, I'm pretty excited to see Samardzija get called up again.  He was a pleasant and somewhat unexpected surprise last year as he played the role of one of those guys that seems to put up better numbers each time he moves up.  He pitched in 26 games last year and posted a 2.28 ERA with the Cubs.

I was glad to see Samardzija optioned to Iowa to start the season.  I think he'd make a great starter.  In his short stint in Iowa last year as a starter, he went 4-1 in 6 games.  He does need to develop that third pitch, however.  But the Cubs bullpen scares me right now and I think it's a good idea to bring him in.  I have no reason to believe this, but I think it might excite the bullpen a little.  If nothing else, it might show then that Lou and the Cubs aren't afraid to make some tough or unexpected moves to keep this club winning games.

Vizcaino is apparently out.  I'm guessing Cotts is breathing a little easier today knowing that he is staying...for now.  If there is a member of that bullpen that has not performed, it's Cotts.  I would say the general consensus is that Cotts' saving grace was the fact he's the only guy in the bullpen that can throw with his left arm.  Although, I'd be willing to bet that Marmol could throw more strikes with his left arm than Cotts has been lately.
So what do you think?  Do you think Vizcaino is the right guy to send packing?   He's only given up 2 hits in nearly 4 innings.  The bigger question, do you think the bullpen settles down enough for Samardzija to go back to Iowa or is he here for the duration?  I don't remember exactly, but I don't think it was expected that Samardzija would finish out the season last year.  I seem to remember it originally being a temporary thing and he ended up pitching in 26 games.

Chime in with your opinions on this move.
