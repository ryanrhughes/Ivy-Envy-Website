After drafting what many considered to be the best college bat available in the first round, the Cubs began the expected stockpiling of pitchers in the 2nd round.

With the 45th overall pick, the Cubs selected RHP, Jake Stinnett a senior from Maryland. He was drafted in 2013 by the Pirates (29th round) but chose to not sign.

Stinnett is 6'4", 215 lbs. and features a fastball, slider and change-up. His fastball sits at 92-94mph and has good movement. He projects to be a #3/#4 starter. Stinnett had time in the infield and as a reliever before becoming a starter in 2013. This means he has less wear-and-tear and pitches on his arm than your typical college senior.

Like the selection of Kyle Schwarber in the 1st round, it's expected that the Stinnett would sign and under-slot deal, giving the Cubs more flexibility in later rounds. Baseball America had Stinnett ranked #67.
