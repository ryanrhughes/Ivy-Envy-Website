It feels like God hates the Cubs' pitching staff, and it's not just the at the major-league level.  This wrath is now extending down through the system as well.  Rob Whitenack, who has been one of the best pitchers in the system this year, is going to have to have Tommy John surgery.  Splitting his time between Daytona and Tennessee this season, Whitenack has posted a 7-0 record and an ERA of 1.93.  Although Whitenack hasn't been necessarily considered one of the Cubs' top prospects (8th round pick in '09), he had upgraded his reputation based on his performance this season.

Speaking of top prospects, Trey McNutt has now been placed on the disabled list.  He has dealt with blister problems for most of the season, but this recent DL stint is apparently due to a collision with another player.

The McNutt injury now gives the Peoria Chiefs' RHP Dallas Beeler a shot at the double A level.  With all of the injuries and the performance issues with the big-league staff, I expect the Cubs to go after plenty of pitching in the upcoming draft.

In case you haven't been paying attention for a while (and I don't blame you if you haven't), the Smokies' DJ LeMahieu was called up to the Cubs the other day.  He hasn't made too much of an impact--he has one start at third for Ramirez, and he has one hit so far.
