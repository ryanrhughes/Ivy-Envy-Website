This year, Ivy Envy is expanding coverage of the Cubs' farm system. We're conveniently situated about halfway between Chicago and Des Moines and less than 100 miles from Peoria. Our goal is to take in as many games as we can in person and bring you the latest on future Cubs. We probably won't be traveling to Tennessee or Daytona, but we'll keep tabs on those teams as well. I'll be doing my best to keep you up to speed on the Iowa Cubs.

Today is opening day for the Pacific Coast League Iowa Cubs and they'll be starting their season vs. the Round Rock Express in Round Rock, TX.

The I-Cubs have a few names you probably recognize—starting pitchers will be Casey Coleman (once he gets back from Chicago), Thomas Diamond, J.R. Mathes, Austin Bibens-Dirkx and Robert Coello—right handed relievers Justin Berg, Esmailin Caridad, Chris Carpenter, Jake Muyco, and Jeff Stevens along with lefties John Gaub and Scott Maine. The I-Cubs have two quality catchers Max Ramirez and Chris Robinson. Position players Matt Camp, Scott Moore, Bobby Scales, Bryan LaHair and Ivy Envy favorite Fernando Perez round out the roster. A total of 14 of their players are on the 40 man roster. As we've seen with the Cashner/Wells injuries and subsequent Coleman call-up, we'll being seeing a few of these guys in Chicago during the season as well in September when the rosters expand. Chris Carpenter, the Cubs' #6 prospect according to Baseball America, seems to be on the fast track moving from rookie league to AAA in just three full seasons.

Bill Dancy has been promoted from the AA Tennessee Smokies to take over managing duties after the departure of Ryne Sandberg. Sandberg will manage the LeHigh Valley Iron Pigs, the Phillies AAA affiliate. Dancy has 22 years experience managing in the minor leagues, mostly in the Phillies organization.

In 2010, Sandberg led the I-Cubs to a tie for the best record in the PCL at 82-62 and was named PCL manager of the year. Even with the best record in the league they lost their playoff spot to the division winning Memphis Redbirds.

I look forward to following the Iowa Cubs through their season. As the Cubs focus more on cultivating young talent, it'll be nice to have some familiarity with these guys before we see them in Chicago.
