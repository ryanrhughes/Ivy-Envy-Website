I've been putting off this post for a few days, but a Monday night with no Cubs game and a wife that wants to watch The Bachelorette is giving me the 20 minutes it takes for me to construct a post and then go back and attempt to fix all my grammatical errors.

During Lou's presser after Saturday's game, he mentioned an interaction he had with a fan after pulling out Kevin Gregg.  Apparently, the fan criticized Piniella for not pulling Gregg sooner.  Since when did $200 for a good seat and some Old Style give you the right to heckle a MLB player or manager?  I can understand the frustration in the situation.  My couch took more punches in that 20 minutes than it has in it's 30 years of existence...yes, we have hand-me-down furniture.  I have no doubt that all of us were more frustrated in the top half of the 9th inning than we have been all season.  But really, what good does it do to make a fool out of yourself in front of millions of people by calling out the manager in the middle of the game?

I'm not big on the booing of players or managers, either.  If a player is struggling, what good will it do to boo them?  Soto, Lee and Bradley come to mind.  You think you are bothered more by their performances than they are?  No.  Now, if a player does not show effort and...oh...say....gets thrown out at second after going into a homerun trot on contact and then realizing the ball isn't going over the fence , boo away.  That's laziness and worthy of booing.  A conscious decision was made to loaf on the play.

Unless you want this guy showing up at your work and heckling you after you make what he perceives to be a mistake, enjoy the ballgame and shut up.
