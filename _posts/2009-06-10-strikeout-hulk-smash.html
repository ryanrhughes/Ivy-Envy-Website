The Cubs defeated the Astros 7-1 last night behind the strong pitching of Ted Lilly and the resurgence of some dormant bats.  Lilly picked up his 7th win, Mike Fontenot went 4-5, including a solo homerun, and Geovany Soto went 3-5.  The only bat that remained MIA was Milton Bradley's.  He went 0-6, and smashed his bat on the plate in frustration after one strikeout.  After a subsequent strikeout, Bradley peacefully gave the ineffective bat to a fan.  Bradley ended up leaving 10 men on base!  He got a little redemption with a great diving catch in the ninth inning, but it was a rough game for him.  I'm trying my hardest to like Bradley, but he's making it very difficult for me.  Making big strikeout hulk scenes just gives fuel to the haters.  Doesn't he realize that?  He has to, so apparently he has no control over his emotions (I do realize how late I am on that revelation).

Well, as many have already said, last night's game was one of the most enjoyable games to watch so far this season.  I think this week is going to continue to be a nice week to watch Cubs baseball, and then this weekend against the Twins, we have the potential to return to our previously scheduled exercises in futility.  But I hope not.  Harden will be back on Saturday, and on Sunday I will be in Chicago to watch my man Ted destroy.  Hopefully all of those Cubs bats stay alive and thrive.

-Jake Fox was sent back to Iowa after the Cubs activated Aaron Miles.  Wheee.  Now who is it going to be when Harden gets activated?

-The Cubs selected CF Brett Jackson with the 31st pick of the draft yesterday.  Bleed Cubbie Blue posted a highlight video of Jackson.  If you haven't yet checked out Bleed Cubbie Blue, it is a good site for Cubs information, especially if you are interested in the farm system.  They provide regular updates of how the Cubs minor-league players are doing.
