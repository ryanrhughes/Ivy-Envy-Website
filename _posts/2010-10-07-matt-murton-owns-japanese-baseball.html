If you've read the site for a length of time, you'll know that most guys on the Ivy Envy team are big Matt Murton fans.  I feel it's my duty to support, and remind Cubs fans of the feats of success of my fellow pelo rojo.

If you had lost track of Murton, he went to play for the Hanshin Tigers after he was released by the Rockies last December.  Murton has settled into Japanese Baseball real well...at least on the field.  I can't speak to his inclusion into Japanese culture.

Matt made headlines this week by setting the Nippon Professional Baseball record for hits in a season.  What makes this especially note-worthy is the player that he de-throned from this record - Ichiro Suzuki.  Murton got his record-breaking 211th hit in the 2nd inning against the Yakult Swallows on Tuesday and ended the game with 213 hits.

It should be noted that the league's season is 14 games longer now than it was in 1994, when Suzuki set the record.  But it's great to see Murton succeeding in baseball.  He finished the season batting .351 with 17 homeruns and 91 RBI's.
