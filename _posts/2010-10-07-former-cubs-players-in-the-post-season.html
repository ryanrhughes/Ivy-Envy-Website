Unless you are a very new to following the Cubs, you are probably used to not seeing the boys in blue playing in the post-season.  However, the rosters for the teams in the playoffs are typically riddled with ex-Cubs players.  There aren't that many out there this year.

The Yankees have the largest number of former Cubs on their roster.  They also have a manager that many Cubs fans would like to see wearing a different shade of pinstripes.  Kerry Wood has played very well for the Yankees since he headed to New York after being traded by the Indians.  With Cleveland, he had a 6.30 ERA in 23 games.  He has pitched in 24 games for the Yankees and posted a 0.69 ERA in the regular season.  Opponents are only hitting .161 off Kerry since his move.  Sergio Mitre is also playing for the Yankees.  He was in Chicago from 2003-2005 and went 4-9 with the Cubs.

Andres Blanco is playing 2nd base for the Rangers.  The only thing Cubs fans should remember about Blanco is that he wore #13 right before Starlin Castro.

2010 was an odd year for the Cubs and in August, we saw the most steady player of the decade for the Cubs in Derrek Lee traded to Atlanta.  All Cubs fans were disappointed in Lee's production this year and it has increased a little for the Braves.  He batted .251 for the Cubs and in the last month and a half of the season, he batted .287 for the Braves.  He also raised his OPS nearly 100 points from .751 to .849.  Oh yeah, Kyle Farnsworth is on the Braves, too.

Another player that many Cubs fans liked that was traded away in 2010 is Mike Fontenot, who is playing with San Francisco.  In 28 games for the Giants, Little Babe Ruth has batted .282 with 0 HR and 5 RBI's.  2010 may cause Fontenot to lose the nickname, though.  He hit 1 homerun in the regular season.  It was his lowest output since coming up to the Majors in 2007.  His highest homerun totals?  He hit 9 homeruns in both 2008 and 2009 for the Cubs.

Cincinnati and Philadelphia don't have any ex-Cubs on their rosters, which makes them even easier to hate.  Tampa Bay and Minnesota don't either, and that's not surprising considering how those organizations are constructed.

The biggest thing to know about these 2010 playoffs though, is that if you want to continue listening to Bob Brenly's analysis, you'll have to suffer through Dick Stockton's play-by-play announcing.  Unfortunately, Stockton will be doing the Division Series that interests me the most, the Braves vs. the Giants.  I'll be pulling for the Twins and the Giants in the playoffs, which means it will be the Yankees vs. the Phillies.
