The Cubs are ringing in the new year with their biggest move of the off-season.  They signed Marlon Byrd for 3 years and $15 million.  I know very little about Byrd, but I do know that although he is 32 years old, he has really blossomed in the last three years with Texas.  I wonder if Rudy Jaramillo might have pushed for this.  With the Rangers, Byrd has a .295 batting average and in 2009, he had a career year with 20 HR and 89 RBI.  We know how it usually works out signing players after a career year, but this is not a huge financial risk.

When looking at the 2010 Cubs outfield, it pleases me that Milton Bradley is not in the picture and Kosuke Fukudome is back in Right Field.  I do find it pretty humorous that on BaseballReference.com, the most similar batter to Byrd is Reed Johnson.

I would like to see Marlon Byrd put the hurt on the Cardinals a few times this season.
