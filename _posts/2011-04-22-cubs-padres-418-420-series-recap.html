The Cubs entered the series 7-8 and ended the series 9-9.

The Padres entered the series 7-8 and ended the series 8-10.

Monday, April 18th--Cubs 1, Padres 0 (10 innings)

This game was broadcast on WCIU.  Listening to the game on the radio was fine, but it would've been cool to watch Zambrano's 10-strikeout performance live.   I had to settle for the video highlights the next day.  Despite the frigid temperature at Wrigley (34 degrees + 12 mph wind at game time), Zambrano went deep into this game, pitching eight strong innings.  However, the Cubs offense was unable to take advantage of Z's start--Padres' starter Tim Stauffer blanked them through seven innings of work.  It took ten innings and a pinch-hit double by Tyler Colvin to win this game.  We have been bagging on Colvin a lot lately, so it was nice to actually see him get a clutch hit for once.

 

Tuesday, April 19th--PPD due to ridiculous weather.


 

Wednesday, April 20th--Game 1: Cubs 2, Padres 1 (11 innings); Game 2: Padres 5, Cubs 4

Tuesday's gelid weather forced a Wednesday double header, and unfortunately, the weather wasn't that much better on Wednesday.  I tuned into game one just in time to see Reed Johnson hit his walk-off homer.  Johnson later talked about the Padres' Gregerson being basically a two-pitch pitcher, and he correctly predicted a slider with a 1-1 count.  This guy is a smart, hard-playing player who is hard not to like.  Take a look at the replay and watch him fly out of the box after he hits this ball.  The video is also worth watching for the commentators' reactions; Brenly lets out a Santo-esque shout.  Also, at about 25 seconds in, look for Samardzija's amazing celebratory ballet-kick.  The other good news from this game is that Garza finally had a strong outing.  He pitched six scoreless innings and struck out nine.

Game two didn't go as well for the Cubs.  It had already been a long day, and the Cubs were looking for James Russell to pitch as deep into the game as possible.  Unfortunately, Russell gave up four earned runs and only pitched four innings.  Former Bronson Arroyo groupie, Aaron Harang, pitched well for the Dads Club.  He earned the win to get to 4-0 on the season.  He also loves to eat bamboo.

Soriano hit a two-run homer late in the game to make it close, but the Cubs just couldn't finish the rally.

Up Next

The Dodgers Carnival rolls into Chicago for a three-game series with the Cubs.  We here at Ivy Envy are really looking forward to Saturday's Dempster-Lilly matchup, but Kurt said he is more interested in seeing Aaron Miles back in Chicago.  Hopefully Ted Lilly's next paycheck doesn't bounce.  We might have to pass a hat around for him at the next live event.
