Josh Vitters, Ryan Flaherty and the Peoria Chiefs - Ivy Envy ---

Last night, the Single A Cubs affiliate, the Peoria Chiefs came to the Quad Cities to face our hometown River Bandits, which is a Cardinals affiliate.  The Peoria roster is a little more loaded right now than usual.  Josh Vitters and Ryan Flaherty are currently occupying the left side of the infield for the Chiefs.  For those of you not familiar with them, Vitters is the #3 overall 2007 draft pick.  He's considered the #1 prospect in the Cubs farm system.  Flaherty is ranked as the #8 Cubs prospect.  Other notable names with Peoria right now are Chris Carpenter (no, not that one), Chris Archer (came to the Cubs in the Derosa trade) and Michael Brenly (son of Cubs TV broadcaster, Bob Brenly).

I will post some pictures here and mention a few things I saw, but I'll leave most of the analysis to Kurt.   It was a beautiful night for a game.  Any evening game in April that has a game-time temperature of 65 degrees is a good thing.  Before I get into the actual on-field play, I have to share  a few of my off-field experiences.

First, it was "College Night" with $2 drafts.  There were lots of college kids.  It was starting to get more rowdy when I left in the 7th inning.  I think the beer must have been left over from last season because it tasted awful.  We also had this wacky lady sitting behind us that had some great insight into the game.  When Carpenter was pulled, she stated "Oh, it looks like they're bringing in another starter".  However, my favorite situation with her was when she blew a gasket when Josh Vitters took first base after he struck out, but  the ball got away from the catcher.  Vitters ended up safe at first.  She was baffled at how Vitters could take first base after striking out and screamed to the "ref" about how bad he was.

The Chiefs were late to town because of a high school game at Modern Woodmen Park.  Because of this, they did not take batting practice.  I was able to observe the warmups.
