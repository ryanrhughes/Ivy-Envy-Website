I know it's been talked about all day today and the last thing that is needed is another opinion on the matter, but seriously, what an asshat.  Of course, I'm speaking of the guy in the front row in the white shirt.  The second he threw the beer at Shane Victorino, I was completely embarrassed to consider myself a Cubs fan.  That photo right there shows exactly what people think about when they hear "Cubs fans".  We are no longer the loyal supporters of the Loveable Losers, in their eyes.  People think that Cubs fans are massive douchebags hanging out at the only bar in the world that holds 40,000 people.

I'm not saying that everyone that started following the Cubs after 2003 are idiots.  Kurt is the best post-2003 fan I know.  But the vast majority of them are like that little cross-section in the photo.  Look at them all and their shit-eating grins.  I mean, the guy was wearing white arm-bands on both arms.  Yeah, it's a hot August evening in Chicago, but do your arms really sweat that much?

The fact is, Bartman acted without thinking and did something that many, if not most, Cubs fans would do.  What this guy did was not a reaction.  It was an aggressive douchey thing he did to make his frat buddies laugh.

It was all easier when the Cubs sucked and everyone knew they suck.  It was embarrassing to be a Cubs fan.  It showed a lack of judgement.  I miss those days.

In case you haven't heard, come hang out with us non-douchebag Cubs fans at Twenty Sports Grille in Moline on August 25th during the Cubs vs. Nationals game.  We promise that a guy won't throw beer on you.
