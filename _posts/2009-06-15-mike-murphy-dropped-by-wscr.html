I flipped on The Score today to listen to Murph get the wheels of the Crosstown Series rolling.  Instead I heard "The Danny Mac" show, hosted by Dan McNeil.  I am not familiar with him, but apparently he's been around in Chicago sports talk for a while.

I've always had mixed feelings about Murph.  I can't stand all the in-show plugs and advertisements.  I haven't liked his tendency to pick one Cubs player and just rail on him until he's run out of town.  He did this to Jacque Jones and I feel that without Jacque, the Cubs wouldn't have been in the 2007 playoffs.  He also seemed a little off-kilter.  He sounded and spoke like he was much older than he actually is.  He sounded uneducated, but that was nice considering he was right before Boors and Bernstein and their always-enlarging egos and inside jokes.  Overall though, it was a tough show to listen to. I won't miss the Brown's Chicken and Rental Max plugs.

There were some moments that I really enjoyed in the Mike Murphy show.  John Dewan, in my opinion, provided some of the most interesting sports talk I've ever heard.  Most importantly, he was a guy that I knew I could flip on for some Cubs talk.  The Score has increasingly become White Sox oriented, especially since they started broadcasting White Sox games.  Murph wasn't, at all, what I wanted out of a radio show, but he offered more Cubs talk than anyone else and I appreciated that.
