Results
Pirates win series 2-1.
Monday: Pirates 1 Cubs 0 (10 innings)
Wednesday: Pirates 4 Cubs 3 (16 innings)
Thursday: Cubs 3 Pirates 2

Cubs Overall Record: 1-2

The Good
As Cubs fans, we're pretty familiar with seasons starting with strong starting pitching performances. The Cubs got outstanding outings from Jeff Samardzija, Edwin Jackson and Jason Hammel. Combined, the starting pitching gave up two runs in 19 innings pitched, a .95 ERA.

Nobody expected Emilio Bonafacioto get off to the start he did in this series. How about .688/.706/.813? Although he got picked off twice in the series, I really like what his speed brings to this team.

The Bad
Starlin Castro did not get much work in during Spring Training because of an injury. You would expect Castro to get off to a slow start, and he did. He looked a bit lost at the plate. Castro hit .077/.200/.077 in the series. It might take Castro a while to get up to speed.

I'm sure he appreciated the day off on Thursday after Wednesday's 16 inning game, but Welington Castillo also struggled at the plate in his two games. Castillo hit .100/.182/.100. Obviously, this is not something to be overly concerned with. Two games is about as "small sample size" as it gets.

The Ugly
In recent memory, the Cubs have been awful with Runners In Scoring Position. So far in 2014, they are amazingly bad. In the first two games of the series, the Cubs went 1-27 with RISP.
