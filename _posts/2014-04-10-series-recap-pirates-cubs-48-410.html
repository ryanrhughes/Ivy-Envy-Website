Result
Tuesday: (L) Pirates 8 Cubs 7
Wednesday: (W) Cubs 7 Pirates 5
Thursday: (L) Pirates 5 Cubs 4

Cubs Record: 3-6

The Good
After struggling in 2013, Anthony Rizzo has started 2014 off strong and he had a stellar series against the Pirates. In the three games, Rizzo went 6-8 with two doubles. Rizzo is now hiting .371/.463/.514 on the season.

Speaking of having a rough season in 2013, Starlin Castro was off to a bit of a slow start. I think we can blame it on the fact that he didn't get much game-action in Spring Training because of an injury. In this series, Castro went 7-13 with 2 HR and 5 RBIs. He has raised his batting numbers significantly - .342/.359/.525.

Holy Emilio Bonifacio! He's reached base safely in each of the first 9 games of the season. It's been a lot of fun watching a real lead-off hitter. Bonifacio went 5-14 (.357) in the series and is hitting .452 on the season and has 7 stolen bases.

Jason Hammel pitched well (besides three solo HRs) on Wednesday. He went 7 innings and struck out six. The three solo home runs were the only three hits he gave up in the game. Hammel is now 2-0 with a 2.63 ERA.

The Bad
I keep thinking that at some point, Edwin Jackson's luck will change, but it just never seems to happen. Jackson had another rough game on Tuesday, going 4 2/3 innings and surrendering 6 runs on 9 hits. He registered 4 strikeouts, but also walked 4.

The Ugly
While the Cubs signed Jose Veras to close this season, the long-term closer appears to be Pedro Strop. And boy, he sure looks like a Cubs closer. In this series, he pitched 1 1/3 innings and gave up 3 runs, two of which were solo HRs. He also walked two. With Strop showing no reliability out of the bullpen right now, it's probably a good thing that the Cubs brought in Veras...assuming he can find the strike zone.

You want ugly? How about the 7th inning of today's game? Brian Schlitter, James Russell. Ricky went to the bullpen with the Cubs up 4-0 after a great Travis Wood outing and by the end of the inning, Schlitter and Russell had both given up home runs and the Cubs were down 5-4 and ultimately lost a game they should have won. Ugly.
