The Cubs came through last night with a 3-0 victory over the Dodgers.  Kershaw is a tough pitcher and looked pretty sharp last night.  Fortunately, Rafael Furcal's defense didn't.  Kershaw only gave up one unearned run due to a Furcal error, and Lee's 2-run homer was off the bullpen.  Dempster was his usual self last night--the only difference is he didn't make any mistakes late in the game that cost him.  If his pitching performance had been any less dominant, the Cubs could have lost this game.  Gorzelanny will have to have another stellar outing tonight if the Cubs hope to beat Billingsley, who currently is 5-2 with a 3.66 ERA.

-Fukudome has come in four out of the last six games as a defensive replacement late in the game.  I know Piniella is trying to get players like Nady some playing time (and it seems to have helped Nady), but in doing so, there is a chance that Fukudome's bat could cool down.  He is still hitting over .300.  I'd like for that to continue.  Oh, and part of the reason I bring this up is that tonight's starter was a righty, so you would expect Fukudome to start if we're so concerned about right-lefty matchups.

-Hey Joe, start Reed Johnson, you knuckleknob!

-How about that collision between Marlon Byrd and James Loney last night?  I was surprised Loney's arm didn't snap off.  Marlon Byrd is in my top 5 of players I would never want to have a collision with.  Who is #1 you ask?  Ted Lilly.

-How about Edgar Tovar's snag on a screaming foul ball near the bullpen?  I'm guessing this Baseball-Reference link is the same Edgar Tovar.  Do you ever wonder about the life of the bullpen catcher like I do?  It seems like it would be a nice gig, especially after a baseball career that never took off.  Check out the comments on this page.  Ouch.

So anyway, if the Cubs can keep up the fire, we might see .500 soon!  The upcoming schedule wants to dampen my enthusiasm, but I won't let it, damnit!
