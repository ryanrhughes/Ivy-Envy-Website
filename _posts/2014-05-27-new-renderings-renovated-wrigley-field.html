Last week, the Cubs and Tom Ricketts released a video that informed us of their intentions to push forward with Wrigley renovations. Today, new renderings of the updated renovations plans were released. This is a 4-year renovation plan.


Renderings of the new plan for Wrigley's outfield. 7 signs total including 2 video boards: pic.twitter.com/8Sp7PHt8eq

— Danny Ecker (@DannyEcker) May 27, 2014

Also note new light towers in LF and RF. And no more lights on LF video board: pic.twitter.com/1o8vLIzkSB

— Danny Ecker (@DannyEcker) May 27, 2014

Rendering of RF bleachers with 3 extra rows of seats and bullpen underneath: pic.twitter.com/GAsaaqKX7A

— Danny Ecker (@DannyEcker) May 27, 2014

You can't help but notice the (lack of) views from the rooftops in these images. After hearing that the bullpens would be under the bleachers, it appears that the wall, and more importantly ivy, will remain relatively untouched.

Also, Bruce Miles tweeted that the Cubs will break ground on the new clubhouse in mid-July.
