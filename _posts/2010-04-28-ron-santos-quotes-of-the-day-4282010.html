Day games in the Spring means that I forced to listen to the radio broadcasts of Cubs games. At some point, we will have a discussion on the podcast about broadcasters. It’s a conversation that is long overdue. I am not a Ron Santo fan. I understand why people enjoy listening to Ron, but I like a little analysis from my color commentary. But since I listened to the radio broadcast, I’d like to throw out a couple of Ron’s quotes from today’s game. If you listened to the game, I’m sure you cocked your head in confusion just as I did when he uttered them.

“We can’t ill-afford to leave a runner on base.”

“Wil Nieves – his real name is Wilbert, and that’s short for Wil.”

With that last quote, Judd Sirott seemed stunned and speechless. Judd is not nearly as seasoned as Pat Hughes in maneuvering Ron through a conversation. Pat is the only tolerable aspect of the radio broadcast and Judd just really isn’t that good. Neither he nor Ron can describe a play. I had no idea what Marlon Byrd did in the field that both described as one of the finest plays they’d ever seen and would surely be the top play of the day in baseball. All I could figure out is that it involved a back-handed catch and maybe it was behind him.
