After his 7-strikeout performance on Sunday that improved his record to 12-8 (8-1 at Wrigley), Ted Lilly said, "I take pride in what I do.  That's for sure.  It's really important to me.  This is a pretty special opportunity I have, not only to play Major League Baseball, but to be able to do it here in Chicago.  I think anyone in their right mind would consider themselves pretty fortunate."

You've got to love it when a person who makes a lot of money is grounded enough to remember how fortunate and blessed he/she is to be in that situation.  Lilly sounds grateful in this quotation.  Is there an implication that there are some players surrounding him who might not be in "their right minds?"  Maybe so, but at any rate, some of the other Cubs players should be trying to emulate Lilly's attitude.  When people take pride in their work, they want to work to the best of their abilities.  I think more often than not, that type of effort equates to winning, and Ted Lilly's record since he has been a Cub proves that point.  I think it will take a team full of players who take pride in their work to get the job done.  The TLFC might be on to something with that cloning machine...
