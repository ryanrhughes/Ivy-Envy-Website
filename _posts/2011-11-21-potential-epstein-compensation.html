Carrie Muskat wrote that the Cubs and Red Sox will not come to an agreement on compensation for Theo Epstein until after the Rule 5 draft in December, but plenty of people have offered opinions on the matter.  I saw that Nick Cafardo, a writer from The Boston Globe, thinks that the Cubs should send Tyler Colvin to the Red Sox and "call it a day."  Tyler Colvin has played 221 games (spanning three seasons) for the Cubs, and he has hit .215 with 78 RBI and 163 strikeouts.  I would rather the Sox took Colvin than a younger player.  In fact, unless the Red Sox want to take Soriano and pay most of his contract, I can't think of a much better option.  Maybe there is some mineral in the water in Boston that will help Colvin's eye-hand coordination.  I'd be interested to hear what anyone else reading this thinks.

Things have been fairly quiet since the Cubs hired Sveum.  Some people have been asking Sveum if he is going to push the management to go after Prince Fielder, but he has kind of brushed those questions aside.  It is fairly safe to assume that the Cubs are not going for a big-name free agent.  According to the same Cafardo article, Epstein will "listen to offers on Garza and just about anyone on his team."  Though this statement appears to be just Cafardo's insight into the Cubs' situation, Epstein and Hoyer have repeatedly mentioned how they want to focus on building up the system.  If they are considering putting any player on the block, it sure would've been nice if Hendry would've done a little more before the trade deadline last season.  At least Epstein is going to have a lot more ownership over what this team will become due to Hendry's inaction.  It is possible, I suppose, that Hendry's hands were more tied than we imagined due to lack of interest from other teams.

The only other news worth noting really is that the Cubs placed Jeff Beliveau, Junior Lake, Matt Szczur and Josh Vitters on the 40-man roster.  I'm happy for Szczur.  He seems like a hardworking and talented player.  It looks like it's getting closer to sink or swim time for Vitters.
