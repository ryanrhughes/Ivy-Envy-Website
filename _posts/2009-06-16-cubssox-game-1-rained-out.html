Well, now that I am done for work for 2 months, I hope to be able to see more games and follow the daily ups and downs more closely.  At this point, there is no make-up date for game one of this series.  Let's hope it's scheduled a little better than the Cubs one game (at night) stop in Atlanta on Monday before heading up to Detroit.  That Chicago-Atlanta-Detroit trip is similar to about 90% of the airline routes out of Moline International Airport.  Oh, you want to go from Moline to Cleveland?  How about a layover in Memphis, TN?  Care to fly from Moline to Atlanta?  Sure, we'll route you through Minneapolis.  I once flew from Moline to Los Angeles and had layovers in Chicago, Denver and Las Vegas...yeah, on one flight.

Anyway, the rainout today might be good for the boys in blue.  I was pretty nervous about the Cubs sending Soriano and Bradley to a soggy outfield.  The Cubs don't need anymore injuries.  I'm just glad I didn't have tickets and made the trip up to Chicago, find parking and sit down only to have it announced just after gametime that the game would be postponed.  All I did was drive a few blocks down to Twenty and have a beer.
