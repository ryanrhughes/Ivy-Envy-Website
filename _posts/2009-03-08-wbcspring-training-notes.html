Yesterday I settled in for nice long day of baseball.  The World Baseball Classic was on, and the Cubs-Brewers matchup was actually being televised as well.  It was going to be a good day, and not surprisingly, the WBC games ended up being the most exciting part of the day.

Cubs-related moments--

-Marmol pitched pretty well.  He showed a little bit of wildness in his one-inning outing, but he settled in nicely, striking out three straight after hitting the first batter he faced.  He looked like he had a very live arm; his fastball had good velocity and he had some good movement on his breaking pitches.  He still has to have the closer spot locked down over Goggles McFirstnames.

-I saw a couple shots of DeRosa and Lilly hanging out in the dugout and got a little sentimental.  Peavy struggled a little in his start, and I found myself wishing that TMFL would've started instead.

-Soto played pretty well in PR's first game.  Though he did strand some runners, he also drove in a run and drew a walk.  It was strange to see him as DH and not behind the plate calling the game.

-Fukudome has walked four times in two games, and that's about all I have to report on him.  I wish that guy would start hitting.

The good parts of the Cubs-Brewers game:

-It was a chance to see many of the Cubs young pitchers, and they looked pretty good overall; they didn't give up any runs after Zambrano left the game, and David Patton pitched especially well.

-That's about it.

The bad parts of the Cubs-Brewers game:

-The Cubs have now dropped 7 out of their last 8 spring training games after their strong start.

-Reed Johnson's facial hair is more robust than ever.  It is a slight improvement over the way-too-manicured chin strip (emphasis on "slight").

-Soriano can still be pretty annoying to watch.  He can also be pretty exciting to watch, but that side of him didn't show up too much yesterday.

-The lack of offense.

And I'm spent.
