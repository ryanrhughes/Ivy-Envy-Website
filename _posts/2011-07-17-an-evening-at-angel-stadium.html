Forgive my non-Cubs related post today.  But honestly, do you WANT to read about yesterday's 13-3 loss to the Marlins?

A few weeks ago, I was able to attend a Dodgers vs. Angels game in Anaheim.  I spoke a bit about this on the podcast and wanted to share a few photos from the game.

While I know a few very nice Dodgers fans, for the most part, their fan base holds the title of the worst fanbase in the world (according to me).  They are an awful group.  If you think the White Sox fan base is bad, go to a Cubs/Dodgers game in LA and wear a Cubs shirt.  Last year, we had my friend, Gus, (Dodgers season ticket holder) on the podcast and he agreed with about all of my opinions on fan base.  Because of my experiences, the Giants fan beat-down earlier this year did not surprise me at all.

My father-in-law (life-long Southern Californian) said that Dodger Stadium is probably the safest place in LA because of the police presence in response to that incident.  When my mother-in-law aked "Gene has tickets to a Dodgers/Angels game, do you want to go?"  My first response was "Is it in LA or Anaheim?"  Her answer was "Anaheim", so I was down.   I don't care how "safe" Dodger Stadium is now, I don't want to go back.

Angel Stadium is an great venue.  I love watching ballgames there.  While built in a fairly boring stadium era, the Angels have done a great job with upgrades.  They've also done a lot to make it a fan-friendly joint.  Their team shops have great prices, with adult t-shirts as low as $10 and hats as low as $7.50.  They also have lots of food options.  I got a huge container (could easily feed 2) of Panda Express for $8.  I am not well versed in the world of the Angels, but I think it's this kind of fan-centered policies that probably eased the tension of the Los Angeles Angels of Anaheim name-change.  In 2009, the Angels were ranked the most fan-friendly of the 122 pro sports organizations.

I would say the fans were split 50-50 or maybe 60-40 favoring the Angels. Overall, the Dodgers crowd was pretty typical.  They were loud, obnoxious and borderline intimidating.  We had some mellow Dodgers fans sitting in front of us, so it was a pleasant experience at "The Big A" for myself and members of my family.  If you follow us on Twitter, during the game, I sent out the following photo with the caption "Dodgers Fan Hat-Fail".

Earlier in the season, I wrote a post about the Angels giving away Lucha Libre wrestling masks.  This put his on for the final out of the game.  I like the tradition he's started more than "Go Cubs Go".

Before the game, I was walking around the stadium with my brother-in-law, Jay.  While on the outfield concourse, we saw this sign:

That's right, after the game, there would be a Homerun Derby between Angels legend, Tim Salmon, and Jose Canseco.  At the both time, we both said "We are staying for this."  Sure enough, after the game, out wandered Jose Canseco.


This was a softball HR Derby, but as it turns out, Jose Canseco can still hit the hell out of the ball.  Sure, the pitches are being lobbed in and everything, but his swing was pretty fierce.  Here's a video I took of Canseco practicing.  Notice the "warm reception" Canseco receives when he was announced.  I also noticed that, the whole time, he was being ignored by the other former players on the field.



It was a good night at Angel Stadium.  It was cool to be able to spend some time with the guys of the family and take in a game. I've said this before, but if you get the chance to take in a game there, you won't be disappointed.
