In yesterday's podcast, we discussed the Cubs chances of winning the division or the wildcard.   I said something to the effect that I didn't think the Cubs would make it in the playoffs unless they show some urgency.  I believe it was Andy that asked me what that exactly means.

Showing a sense of urgency or hunger to win is not loafing it to second base on a hit off the wall and getting thrown out like Milton Bradley did.  It is also not loafing it around third and barely scoring like Ryan Theriot did.  This is precisely why I am not confident with this team.  I can't think back to a team that showed such little effort or hustle.  Do they still think this division is a cake-walk, even though it's August and they are not in first place?

It seems that every Cubs fan I've talked to in the last week is falling more in love with Bob Brenly and his willingness to publicly call these players out on their lack of effort.  I think he's a good broadcaster and I can only hope that the next manager for the Chicago Cubs will demand that the players earn their inflated salaries.
