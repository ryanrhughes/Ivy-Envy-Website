Apparently, I was not the only person with this concept on my mind today.  On my way home from work, I was listening to The Score, WSCR 670AM and Terry Boers and Dan Bernstein were discussing this very same issue. 

According to them, this is mainly a Chicago Cubs fan phenomenon.  They stated that it is only Cubs fans that live and die on every game and predict the rest of the season based on a game or series. 

Maybe it's because we've seen it all go wrong so many times.  Those painful memories come flooding back to us and we can only assume that the same heartache is just around the corner.

They traced it back to, and blamed it on, Chip Caray.  According to these guys, it was as if he was broadcasting the apocolypse.  I don't remember that, but I didn't get to see many Cubs games during his era due to work/college/living in other parts of the country without cable

All I know is that if Boers and Bernstein are correct, it's just further proof to me that the Cubs have helped define who I am.  For better or for worse, usually the latter, they have become part of me.
