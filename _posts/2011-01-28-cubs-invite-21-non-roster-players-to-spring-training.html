Today, the Cubs signed Braden Looper and Augie Ojeda to minor league contracts that include invitations to Spring Training.  Most Cubs fans will remember Looper for his years with the Cardinals.  Looper last pitched in 2009 for the Brewers.  He posted a 14-7 record despite an ERA of 5.22.  The Brewers did not pick up his option for the 2010 season.

Ojeda, a utility infielder that spent the first four season of his career with the Cubs, is a career .234 hitter.  The only sense I can make out of this move is that the Cubs feel they could use Ojeda's protection against hazardous remote control airplanes during spring training.

Looper and Ojeda will join Reed Johnson and Todd Wellemeyer as veteran players fighting the young talent from the Cubs system for spots on the roster.  There are quite a few names that Cubs fans have been hearing about for a few years.  Spring Training will give us a chance to see guys like Chris Carpenter, Jay Jackson, Trey McNutt, Brett Jackson and Josh Vitters display their talent against some (at times) major league talent.  Spring Training is never a great opportunity to judge the players because they are often facing the up-and-coming young talent of the opposing clubs.

The Cubs have also invited apparently the only player they could find with as much minor league experience as invitee Bobby Scales.  The Cubs have invited 12 year minor league veteran Scott Rice.  All that the lefty pitcher Rice lacks is Scales' 61 games of major league experience.

Other players invited to the 2011 Spring Training are:  James Adduci, Steve Clevenger, Matt Camp, Angel Guzman, Brian LaHair, Luis Montanez, Scott Moore, Chris Robinson, Marquez Smith and Brad Snyder.
