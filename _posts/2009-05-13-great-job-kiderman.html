"A high deep drive... Bobby Scales with his first career homer!" 

Last night's game was pretty great, and the highlight for me was seeing Bobby Scales hit that home run.  He has spent over 10 seasons in the minors prior to this first stint in the majors, and he has worked as a substitute teacher in the off-season to supplement his minor-league income.  It feels great to see a guy like that succeed.  I saw on SportsCenter today that Bobby Jenks makes 6500-some dollars per pitch (he was fined $750 for throwing behind Kinsler--lol).  Imagine how much money players like Soriano or Bradley make for every swing of the bat.  After the ball sailed into the bleachers, Scales sprinted around the bases, and I said, "Slow down and revel in the moment!"  My wife replied, "Maybe that is his way of reveling in the moment."  Keep it rolling, Bobby!

Some other Cubs miscellany:

-Fukudome quietly had another great game, and it is very nice to see the Cubs get some extra-base hits. 

-Freel's quotation when asked how it feels to be now playing for the Cubs: "Thank ya Jesus!"  He went on to say, "If there was any more happiness, there'd have to be two of me."  Wow, is playing in Baltimore that bad?  Have fun, Joey.

-Zambrano is going to do a rehab start in single A.  Can you imagine being one of those guys who has no prayer of making it to the majors and batting against Zambrano?
