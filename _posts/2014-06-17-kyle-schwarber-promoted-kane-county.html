With their first selection (4th overall) in the 2014 MLB Draft, the Cubs selected Indiana University catcher, Kyle Schwarber. Less than two weeks later, Schwarber has not only already signed a contract with the Cubs, but he is with Boise (Class A - Short Season) and absolutely dominating pitching. The deadline for MLB teams to sign 2014 draft picks is July 18th, but Schwarber wasted no time getting into the Cubs system.

Boise started their season on Friday and five games in and after a 2 HR performance today, Schwarber is hitting (a silly) .600/.625/1.350 with 4 home runs. Yes, 4 home runs in his first 5 games of professional baseball.

It's no surprise that Schwarber is hitting Northwest League pitching the way he is. After a college career, Schwarber is advanced for the league.

This evening, Jordan Bernfield tweeted this:


Source tells me #Cubs 1st round pick Kyle Schwarber called up to Kane County from Boise.

— Jordan Bernfield (@JordanBernfield) June 18, 2014

This promotion comes as no surprise. Kris Bryant played just two games in Rookie ball before being promoted to Boise for 18 games and then to High-A Daytona for the final 16 games of the 2013 season. I believe the Cubs want to move these college guys along early to get them at a level that will provide a challenge. You know, that challenge that Kris Bryant hasn't encountered yet.

If I had to guess though, I think Schwarber finishes 2014 with Kane County. This is a team that has already earned a playoff spot and last year and it does not appear that Daytona will be in the post-season if they continue playing the way they have been. We learned that the front office likes the top prospects to get post-season experience. Kris Bryant jumped a non-playoff team in Kane County in 2013 to get on the Tennessee roster for the Southern League playoffs.

If Schwarber finishes 2014 with Kane County and progresses well in the off-season and in Spring Training, perhaps we'll see him jump High-A Daytona and go straight to AA Tennessee.

Like any Cubs fan in the midwest, I'm happy to see Schwarber moved up to the Midwest League. My first opportunity to see him play will probably be for our Quad Cities meet-up on July 8th when Kane County visits our hometown QC River Bandits. There are still spots available if you'd like to join us.
