Fans of 24 baseball teams were faced with a decision this week.  Who do you root/pull for in the playoffs?  I know some fans stop watching when their team's season ends.  I know this type of fan.  I'm not this type of fan.  Going into the playoffs, I told folks (and I mentioned it on the podcast) that I would be pulling for the Cardinals in the NL.  I have reasons behind it.  The Cardinals are in the NL Central.  I would consider that to be "my" division.  They are also a smaller-market Midwest team, and as a baseball fan, I have an appreciation for this type of team.  They are a step down from the Twins in my book.

I realized that it would be impossible for most Cubs fans to pull for the Cardinals.  When the White Sox won the World Series in 2005, I had a list of reasons that I would be pulling for the White Sox.  Yet, as the playoffs progressed, I couldn't do it.  In the middle of a game, I would find myself cheering when the other team scored.  This week, four years later, I realized why it was I could not pull for the White Sox.  What kept me from following through is this:  Ozzie Guillen is a complete asshat and as a team, the White Sox were not likeable at all.  I did not want to see these douchebags succeed.

As the Cardinals are now one game from elimination, I've struggled all week with the idea of whether or not I could root for them.  I felt that I should because of the reasons I listed above.  But I can't root for them.  I can't pull for them.  I can't wish them well in the playoffs.  They are the NL Central, but there is something larger than that loyalty.  It's the fact that if they do well, it gives all the Cardinals fans more ammunition to use against me.  Granted, they have all the ammunition they need when it comes to successful baseball in comparison to the Cubs.  But they need to experience some of the pain we've endured the last few years.  They need to get swept in the first round by the Dodgers.  Our offense was non-existent and we were never in the games in the last two playoff appearances.  The Cardinals had a game won and the guy that played a large role in getting them to the playoffs took a flyball off his nuts and they lost the game.  The heartbreak I saw on the faces of Cardinals fans on Friday morning gave me a great pleasure.

May they lose, and lose painfully.
