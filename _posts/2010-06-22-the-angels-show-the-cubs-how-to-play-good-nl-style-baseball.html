Before Friday's game, I really felt that if the Cubs could put together a solid series, they could work their way into the NL Central race make me feel better about this team.  I've mentioned this many times, but the Angels are my 2nd favorite team.  I've been looking forward to this series all season.  But man, the Cubs are really doing everything they can to push me away.

Friday's game was just painful to watch.  Watching the Cubs commit errors and mental mistakes is hard enough, but when they do it against one of the best fundamental teams in baseball, it really stands out.  I believe Pat Hughes said that the Angels are #1 in the majors at going 1st to 3rd on a base-hit.  This is what I want the Cubs to be.  I think I can handle the Cubs losing if they played good and fundamental baseball.  Of course, good fundamental baseball leads to winning...so yeah.

The Cubs put together a rally in the 9th with homeruns by Colvin and Lee.  For some reason, this only pissed me off more.  I knew they'd come up short.  Situations like that aren't a rush for me this year.  I know they won't end well.  Don't try to get my hopes up.  Interestingly, J from Garden of Halos told us on the last podcast that the Angels bullpen is awful and if the Cubs could get into the Angels bullpen, they would have a chance, regardless of the score.

You might think that Sunday's slaughter of the Angels would draw me back in.  It doesn't - it makes me hate them more.  How dare they put up a 12-spot in the 3rd game of an awful series?  I am so tired of seeing L-L-W in a series, then they don't carry anything over to the next series.

So I feel I have three options with the Cubs at this point in the season, and in my life.

Option #1: Continue rooting on the boys in blue every day - living and dying (mostly dying) with every pitch, batter, inning and game.

Emotions from taking this approach: Frustration, Anger, Depression

Option #2: Give the Cubs the old "it's not you, it's me" talk.  Thank them for their 25 years and be on my way.  I can give a laundry list of reasons and the list ends with the fact they play Zombie Nation at Wrigley Field now.  The idea of leaving the Cubs sounds ridiculous and I've dismissed it anytime I've heard a friend consider it.  However, it's beginning to make sense.

Emotions from taking this approach: Relief, initial 20 lb post-break-up weight gain, then a 30 lb weight loss to appeal to a new team, pleasure from finding a younger and hotter team, years of the ability to look back on a wasted 25 years and see how much better off I am since I broke it off.  And I'll make big money by selling this website.

Option #3: Put 2010 behind me.  Call it a season, enjoy the summer and if I don't happen to have anything to do, maybe I'll turn a Cubs game on.  I'll wait and see what they do to build a winning team and get my hopes up, if their moves warrant that.

Emotions from taking this approach: A care-free spirit this summer, indifference when they get swept by the Pirates.

What it comes down to is this:  What fun is it to be a fan of a team that you don't like?  I think I know what will cure all of this.  In a few weeks, I will see the Cubs play twice in Phoenix and once in LA.  Going to see the Cubs on the road should re-fuel me a little.  For now, it will probably be Option #3, but Option #2 is closer than it's ever been before.
