The Smokies have been playing well as a team.  They are 7-3 in their last ten games and own the best record in the Southern League.  I wish Kodak was closer, so we could see this exciting team play.

Because of the issues with the big league roster, the Smokies have had their share of players joining and leaving the team throughout the season.  Most recently, one of the Cubs' top pitching prospects, Chris Carpenter was moved from Iowa back to the Smokies.  This season, Carpenter struggled a little in the bullpen for Iowa (6.41 ERA), but he pitched fairly well in a starting role for Tennessee for most of last season.  The Iowa Cubs get relief pitcher Blake Parker in exchange.

Injuries are also a pressing concern for the Smokies right now.  They are stretched thin in the outfield with Brett Jackson, Jim Adduci and Ty Wright all on the disabled list.  Jackson has ligament damage in his left pinky finger.  Hopefully these guys can make it back soon.

Offensively, the Smokies are pretty dominant.  Most of the starters are hitting .300-plus.  The one concern is Josh Vitters, who is currently hitting just .257 with a .296 OBP.  Tennessee's pitching is getting the job done for the most part.  They have a 4.10 team ERA which ranks fifth in the Southern League.
