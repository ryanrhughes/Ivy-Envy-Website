I was pretty sure that I covered all of the former Cubs in the post-season in my post last week.  On our Facebook page, Charles was quick to point out that I forgot to mention Dennis Eckersley, who is doing the pre and post game shows on TBS.

I was watching the Giants beat the Braves last night to earn their spot in the NLCS and I immediately recognized a face in the Giants' celebration.  There was Shawon Dunston in the group, enjoying the win.  So I figured I should do a second post, looking at former Cubs on the coaching staffs of the playoff teams.  I am sure someone will find some obscure coach that had a short stint either playing with or on a coaching staff in the Cubs organization, but here's what I have.

The Rangers have no former Cubs on their staff.  Neither do the Twins.  They were eliminated by the Yankees, who have two former Cubs on their staff.  Of course, Joe Girardi is the manager and currently providing a percentage of Cubs fans wet dreams over the idea of him managing the Cubs next season.  The Yankees have a bullpen coach that you might recognize - Mike Harkey.  Harkey played for the Cubs from 1988-1993, and he was the starting pitcher for the first Cubs game I went to in 1990.

The Reds have been eliminated and I'd like to think that former Cubs manager Dusty Baker had something to do with that.   Chris Speier serves as Dusty's bench coach.  Chris was a hitting instructor in the Cubs minor league system in 1995.  He is also the father of Justin Speier, who was drafted by the Cubs in that same year.  I'm guessing that wasn't a coincidence.

The Giants have two former Cubs on their staff.  I mentioned Shawon Dunston, who is officially the Defensive Coordinator.  The great Lee Smith is the Pitching Coordinator for the Giants.

Davey Lopes played with the Cubs from 1984-1986.  He now serves as the first base coach for the Phillies.  Lopes has been a first base coach since 1992 and the Phillies are the six team for which he has served in that capacity.

The Rays have a guy by the name of Don Zimmer as a coach.  You might recognize his name.  He's been in baseball for 62 years.

I'd like to hear some of your memories of these guys from when they wore Cubby blue.
