The Cubs have been very active in the trade market today.  Here is a quick summary of their moves so far--

Trade #1
The Cubs sent Scott Feldman and Steve Clevenger to the Orioles for pitchers Jake Arrieta and Pedro Strop.  In addition to the two pitchers, the Cubs received significant international bonus pool money from the Orioles.  Feldman pitched well for the Cubs this season, and his departure will likely make the remainder of this season that much harder to watch.  However, it made a lot of sense for the Cubs to part ways with him as he would've been in line for a much larger contract after this seasonn (he had a 1-year, 6-mil. contract with the Cubs).  Arrieta and Strop are both pitchers who have fastballs that sit in the mid-nineties, and have been described by multiple sources as being guys who perhaps just needed "a change of scenery."  Strop will join the Cubs this week, and it sounds like Arrieta will begin in Iowa.  Arrieta did make the Orioles opening-day roster this year, so he would be able to pitch for the Cubs if they trade any more of their current rotation.  Including Clevenger in the deal seemed like almost a gift to him since he is from Baltimore originally.

Arrieta this season-- 5 starts, 7.23 ERA, 8.7 K/9, 6.5 BB/9

Strop this season--29 app, 7.25 ERA, 9.7 K/9, 6 BB/9

 

Trade #2
In a trade with the Dodgers, the Cubs exchanged Carlos Marmol for veteran RHP Matt Guerrier.  The Cubs also gave the Dodgers their fourth international bonus slot.  Guerrier is a 34-year-old, right-handed reliever.  In 34 appearances this year, Guerrier had a 4.80 ERA with 6.3 K/9 and 3.6 BB/9.  Both Marmol and Guerrier had been DFA'd by their respective clubs.

 

Trade #3
The Cubs gave the Astros 2B Ronald Torreyes (who had been playing in AA Tennessee) in exchange for the Astros' two and three international bonus slots.  Torreyes was a good hitting prospect but apparently doesn't hit for power at all.  On the podcast, we've talked about how the Cubs have a number of infield prospects who can potentially hit for average and power.

 
Len Kasper pointed out on Twitter that if people focus on the players obtained in these trades, they would be "missing the [point]."  The important part of these trades is all the international bonus pool money that the Cubs obtained.  The Cubs gained $963,000 in money that they will use to secure even more young talent.  Hoyer told Paul Sullivan that the within the group of international players available now, there is a lot of "impact talent."  The Cubs have already begun to obtain multiple international prospects, and we will address that topic in a separate post.
