Milton Bradley lived up to his reputation in his first at-bat at Wrigley Field on Thursday.  Two questionable calls in his at-bat led to an argument with home plate umpire, Larry Vanover.  I was unable to watch the game and had to rely on MLB Gameday on my work computer.  I was pretty baffled when I opened it up and saw that Milton Bradley had been ejected, considering he was injured.  All I could picture was Bradley coming out of the dugout to argue with an ump.  After I did a little research, I realized he had actually taken an at-bat.

Since I did not see the full situation, I can't speak with great authority.  I saw the pitches on tape and they were obviously questionable.  I understand Bradley's ejection given baseball's rule that you can't argue balls and strikes.  However, Bradley's suspension and undisclosed fine seems pretty ridiculous to me.  Apparently, Bradley made contact with Vanover.  Granted the camera angle is not great, but it sure doesn't look to me like Bradley made contact with him.

I was actually impressed that Bradley walked away as soon as he did considering everything we've heard about his temperament.  It appeared as though he had some thing to say, said it and walked away.  This situation does not trouble me.  Those were pretty questionable calls and I think any player except the robotic Fukudome would have had some sort of reaction.  Bradley looked like a guy that wanted to come through for his team and didn't feel like he got a fair opportunity to do that.  For his sake, I hope most Cubs fans appreciate his fire for his team in this situation and don't pile on him.
