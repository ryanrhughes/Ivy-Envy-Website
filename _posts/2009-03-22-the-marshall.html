Yesterday, Sean Marshall pitched five strong innings in the Cubs' 13-2 victory over the White Sox.  It is nice to beat the Sox any time, but like I've said before, I don't give much credence to preseason games.  It was nice to see some of the strong performances, however.

-Our 5th man Marshall seems to be ready to go--yesterday he gave up five hits over five innings and struck out three.  He is 3-0 in spring training with a 1.40 era in 19.1 innings pitched.

-Koyie Hill is having a good spring.  He collected two hits yesterday and continues to make a case for jettisoning Bako into space.

...is also finally making an impact.  He has 9 hits in 23 AB, and yesterday he hit his first homer in spring training.  I am pro-Bradley; he will be good for this team.  Even though Milton Bradley made Trouble, Conspiracy, and Where's the Beef?, they also made Perfection, Battle Masters, and KerPlunk (that's a good noise in baseball, right?).

-Jake Fox is playing well, but what do we do with him?

-DeRosa is going to play 1st base for Team USA today.  He did play 1st base once for the Cubs when Lee was injured.  He is probably doing everyone's taxes as well.  We want you back, Mark.

Only 15 days 'til opening day!  Expect a new Ivy Envy podcast soon.
