Well, we thought we were just getting Theo, and now we have Jed Hoyer and Jason McLeod as well.  The Cubs decided not to just hire a new GM.  They decided to revamp the infrastructure with a group of proven winners.  Hoyer is now the GM, and McLeod will head up scouting and player development.   And now we fans have the immense pleasure of sitting back and either watching the magic happen or the plane crashing into the mountain.  Most fans seem to think that it will be the former.  I agree, but I hope people are willing to accept that it possibly isn't going to be microwave-fast. 

The philosophy of these guys is scouting/player development first.  They want to spend more resources in this area, and it seems to be a wise move.  This year, the Cubs finally put more capital into the draft than they had in a while.  In Carrie Muskat's article on Cubs.com, Epstein is quoted as saying that the Cubs' approach in this year's draft was "a significant moment [for the organization]," and that the Cubs were "finally getting it."  We were thinking that the new management would have to turn the ship around completely, but it appears that the Ricketts already were spinning the wheel. 

These guys are great hirings.  All three were involved in creating the Red Sox teams that won the World Series in '04 and '07.  It sounds like Hoyer was partly responsible for Schilling coming to the Sox for the '04 season.  He also was responsible for aqcuiring Josh Beckett and Mike Lowell for Hanley Ramirez and Anibal Sanchez.  Those players ended up being vital to the Sox in the '07 playoffs. 

Now they are charged with the task of taking the Cubs to the promised land.  They have to make a mountain out of twisted, smoldering Hendry-wreckage.  It won't be easy.
