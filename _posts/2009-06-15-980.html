A swing and a miss by Harris!

Yesterday, my wife and I got to go to see the final game of the Cubs-Twins series, and it was the first time we have taken a bus trip to a game.  The Cubs are now 3-0 in games I have attended.  I realize the fact that I have only been to three games makes me sound like a bad fan, but in my defense, we do live three hours away from Chicago.  Overall, I would recommend a bus trip since it removes the annoyance of driving and finding a parking space.  It also allows you to watch your favorite cinematic masterworks such as The Gameplan and Kicking and Screaming (of course I am making fun of the Will Ferrell movie, not the superior Noah Baumbach movie with the same title).  However, the first movie of the trip was Rookie of the Year, and I thoroughly enjoyed it.  The Cubs could use a Henry Rowengartner in their bullpen now.

I thoroughly enjoyed the game as well.  I was aching for the Cubs to take this game and avoid the sweep.  I knew having Lilly on the mound would help; it would just be a matter of stringing together some hits.  And they did that in the bottom of the ninth.  Lee's gutsy baserunning ended up winning the game for the Cubs, and time slowed down for me when Theriot hit his game-winning single.  I can see the moment vividly in my head even now--our vantage point was perfect.  The ball sliced off of Theriot's bat, and I thought Punto was going to be able to stretch far enough to get a glove on it.  It seemed like slow motion as the ball breezed by the outstretched web of Punto's glove.  As the ball found its way to the outfield, time was back on track, and we were all yelling and exchanging high-fives.  It reminded me of the passage in Of Mice and Men that reads, "As happens sometimes, a moment settled and hovered and remained for much more than a moment.  And sound stopped and movement stopped for much, much more than a moment."

Lilly had a predictably good performance on the mound, and Soto and Theriot also had much-needed good games.  Even though Fukudome's stats for the game look bad, he did come very close to hitting two homers.  I was pumped that I got to see Ted pitch.  He has become my favorite Cubs player.  Before the game, he ran along the warning track, greeting the fans, and they cheered him wildly.  It was a great day!
