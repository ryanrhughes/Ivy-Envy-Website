Stabilize the bullpen. ---

I spent some time on baseball reference this morning looking at relief pitching stats, and guess which team leads the National League in “losses in relief?”  That’s right, your Chicago Cubs with 6.  Yes, the offense hasn’t been great or clutch or whatever, but the bullpen is a festering wound, and if we don’t get some major surgery soon, we’re going to lose the patient (the patient being the whole damn 2010 season).  A butterfly bandage is not going to cut it; get me Dr. Jack Shephard!  So it was time for a gutsy move like moving Z to the bullpen temporarily.  It shows that Lou is unwilling to just let this season slide slowly into the abyss.  I see Carlos as a guy who inspires confidence, and maybe those young guys down there need some iron on which to sharpen themselves (the Cubs have the 5th youngest staff in the NL).   I don’t think just moving Gorzelanny or Silva to the ‘pen was going to do much good.  In my mind, Gorzelanny is the same damn pitcher as Marshall.  And Silva appears to be back to being the gaping Kraken maw that swallows innings by the boatload, so why have him pitch only one or two at a time?

Also, Z has the “stuff” that is just not present down in the bullpen (or that has yet to show up).  Plus, the rest of the rotation has shown that they can hold it down without Zambrano.  And if they start to show that they can’t, well, we bring Z back into the rotation.  So settle down, kids… we are just trying to fix a big problem with a part of the machine that appears to be moveable at the moment.
