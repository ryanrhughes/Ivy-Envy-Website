There hasn't been much going on with the Cubs to pull me out of my blogging hibernation.  I hope you can forgive us for the lack of activity on Ivy Envy.  As a Cubs fan, I'm sure you can understand the need to take a little time off from our boys in blue.  It's good for us.  Expect an Ivy Envy podcast episode to be posted tonight or tomorrow, by the way.

But what could bring me out of my hibernation?  Aaron Miles being traded! The man that was brought in to replace Mark DeRosa failed miserably in 2009.  In 74 games, he batted .185 and had 5 RBI.  As far as 2009 Cubs that I did not want to see on the 2010 roster, Aaron Miles was right there behind Milton Bradley.

Miles was traded with Jake Fox to the Oakland A's for Jeff Gray, Ronny Morla and Matt Spencer.  According to Cubs.com, Gray was placed on the 40 man roster.  He had pretty decent middle relief numbers for the A's last year.  I'm sure Cubs fans will have mixed feeling on losing Jake Fox, mainly because of the numbers he put up while in AAA Iowa.  He was on a tear in Iowa and filled in for the Cubs well.

It's hard for me to step back and evaluate if the Cubs got the better end of this deal.  The fact that Miles is gone distorts my perception of the trade itself.  My hunch is that the A's came out ahead.  With Jim Hendry's recent stretch of bad luck, I would imagine that Billy Beane would come out on top in that match-up.
