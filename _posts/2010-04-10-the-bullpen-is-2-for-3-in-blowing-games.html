The Cubs losing baseball games hurts.  The Cubs losing to Dusty Baker really hurts.  The Cubs bullpen blowing a lead late in the game to Dusty Baker...that makes me want to circle Wrigley Field drinking my pain away.  I can handle losses like the one the Cubs were dealt on Opening Day.  These blown games are tougher to swallow.

There are no Cubs fans Esmailin (har har) after this game.  Caridad became a true part of this 2010 bullpen by giving up two walks, an infield hit and a grand slam to allow the Reds to come from behind and take the series opener, 5-4.

There are many frustrating aspects to last night's game:

  • Losing to Dusty Baker
  • Giving up 5 runs on only 5 hits.
  • Yet another blown save.  That's 2 in 4 games.
  • Ramirez and Soriano going a combined 0-9.  Ramirez seemed to be swinging for the fences with every at-bat.  If I remember correctly, he flied out deep twice.
  • Ending the game with the bases loaded and a weak dribbler to the mound by Chad Tracy, after benefiting from two Reds errors, is yet another deflating way for the Cubs to wrap up a game.


There are a few positives to take away from this game:

  • Silva pitched a great game.  He pitched 6 innings and gave up one run off three hits.  Given how Silva pitched, I am hoping that health/injury is not the reason he left the game after 6 innings.  Silva benefited from a very impatient-at-the-plate Reds team and threw only 71 pitches.  I was definitely reminded of the Cubs under Dusty Baker with the way these Reds were hacking early in the count.  He's pitched well and I hope Silva is fine.  After the game, it was reported that he was experiencing some soreness.
  • Derrek Lee had a very un-Derrek Lee-in-April like performance.  Lee went 3-4 with an upper-deck shot in the 9th to bring the Cubs within a run.
  • The Cubs managed 11 hits against the Reds and forced Homer Bailey out after 5 innings with 106 pitches.  I am a fan of patience at the plate.


While the rest of the Cubs blogosphere tells you not to panic, I’m telling you that this bullpen sucks and could very well ruin your summer.  Though I am, admittedly, of the panicky and pessimistic breed.  At work, I am known as the doomsday Cubs fan.  "Eeyore", they call me.  Well, it's interesting that two Cubs were quoted saying the exact same things I have said in the last few days. These are from Carrie Muskat's post-game article.

"It's hard -- we should be 3-1 right now," Silva said. "I think we're playing good enough. This is hard, because later on, you're going to need these wins, especially in this division."

"I hope the games we lost now we don't need in September," Alfonso Soriano said.


After the game, Paul Sullivan, of the Chicago Tribune, tweeted that the Cubs are considering bringing Ted Lilly up after one start in Iowa and moving Gorzellany to the bullpen.  I hope to see some movement from the organization if this bullpen stuff continues.
