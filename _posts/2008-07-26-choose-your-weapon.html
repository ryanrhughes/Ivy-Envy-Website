Ever since the All-Star break, it seems that we never know which bat the Cubs hitters will swing on a particular day.  Too often it has been the ice bat, and quality starts are being wasted.

Harden has 30 strikeouts in his three starts for the Cubs, and he still has yet to get a win.  We need Soriano to heat up again, and hopefully the rest of the lineup will follow suit.  At least there have been plenty of enjoyable defensive highlights in the past few games.  Today Reed Johnson threw himself at another hit in the gap.

Johnson's diving catch

DeRosa also had a great diving catch on Thursday as well.

Derosa's diving catch

And let's not forget Fukudome's catch in the ivy.

Fukudome's ivy catch

Plays like these along with the great pitching have made the past few games great to watch; the Cubs just need to pick up the hot bats.  Tomorrow we might get to see Samardzija pitch again--that guy likes some good music.
