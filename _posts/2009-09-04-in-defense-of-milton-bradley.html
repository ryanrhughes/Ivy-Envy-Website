Milton Bradley has the media and Cubs bloggers abuzz with his recent statements about Cubs fans.  I'm not going to get into the specifics of what he said because I'd just be repeating what's been reported on all over.  I have not had ill feelings towards Milton Bradley all season and I continue to support and defend him, as a player.  I can't really defend him as an individual because I don't know him.

As for his cries of racism, I don't know.  I've never heard anything racist directed at a baseball player and I've never heard anything racist said at Wrigley.  But I doubt a day goes by that I don't hear someone say something racist, so I can't say that it would surprise me that some douche sitting in the right field bleachers would get toasted and in the safety of a crowd and his frat boy buddies and decide to try to get a reaction from the most volatile player on the field.

What I don't understand is why some Cubs fans heckle him the way they do.  I don't think it has anything to do with his performance.  I remember reports from Mesa in March that Cubs fans were heckling him.  If I remember correctly, Bradley performed well in Spring Training.  So what's the deal here?  Do people heckle because they're drunk idiots and want a reaction?  Do people heckle because a guy performs poorly?  I'm not a heckler.  I am not going to hide behind a brick wall with ivy that will protect me from a dude that could snap me in half.  I am not going to heckle a player that is in a hitting slump.  The worse I will do is "boo" a guy that plays lazy.  But it would take quite a bit for me to do that.  So part of what baffles me about it all is that it's just not in my nature to heckle, so I can't get in the head of a heckler.

Milton Bradley is what he is.  He can't keep his cool.  He obviously has a history of feeling victimized.  Why did Cubs fans expect anything different?  Bradley does not come across, to me, as a guy that does not care.  I've seen him run out a routine ground-ball.  I've seen him visibly frustrated when he fails at the plate.  I think he's a guy that cares about his performance and wants to win baseball games.  When he signed with the Cubs, he stated that he does not handle losing well.  The 2009 Cubs aren't a good team for him, then.  It's been a season of disappointment and losing as often as they have won.

So why do fans feel the need to bait him?  He's one of our guys.  He may make a few bone-headed plays, but he shows effort.  He's not having his best season.  But injuries were a huge concern for Bradley.  Last year, he played in 126 games, which was more than he had played in 5 seasons.  He could potentially play more games this year than he ever has in his career.  He's a good baseball player that has had a rough stretch.  The stupid thing is this...he's had a great month.  He's playing well, yet he's heckled by fans.  This leads me to believe the hecklers are idiots looking for a reaction rather than critics of his performance.

Since the All-Star Break, Bradley has raised his Batting Average almost 30 points.  In the month of August, he batted .308 and had an OBP of .427.

Current 2009 Stats:  BA - .266  / OBP - .395  / HR - 12 / RBI - 37 / BB - 66

Career Average:  BA - .279 / OBP - .373 / HR - 11.5 / RBI - 43 / BB - 45

So the way I see it, Bradley is performing pretty closely to how he has throughout his career.  With the way he is hitting the ball, I would expect that his batting average would continue to rise in September and he could be above his career average at the end of the season.  I've heard a lot of people key in on his low RBI total.  I was pretty surprised to see that it's actually pretty consistent with the rest of his career.

I've heard the argument that Milton Bradley is not earning his $7 million.  I have had the opinion in the past that a player needs to earn their salary and I've been upset if a guy gets a huge contract and then their production drops.  Milton Bradley had a career year last year and the Cubs rewarded him with a big contract.  Three years may not seem big, but for Bradley, it is.  They knew his history and they knew his career average numbers, but they obviously hoped he would repeat his 2008 production.

So Milton Bradley had an average season in 2009.  He managed to stay healthy, which is a feat in itself.  But Cubs fans place unrealistic expectations and then heckle him when he is being what he's always been.  It makes no sense to me.  Now if you want to talk about players earning big money and not producing, let's look at Alfonso Soriano.

Soriano is making a cool $17 million.  Let's look at his numbers:

Current 2009 Stats:  BA - .243 / OBP - .305 / HR - 20 / RBI - 55

Career Average:  BA - .279 / OBP - .326 / HR - 31 / RBI - 84

I took out the first two seasons of his career with the HR and RBI because he only played in 31 games between the two seasons.

(By the way, Soriano's BA in August was .185)

So, Soriano makes about 2.5 times Bradley's salary and look at those numbers.  I don't agree with the talk that the Cubs should cut Bradley loose.  According to Tim McCarver on the Fox broadcast on Saturday, there's no way Bradley will be back next year.  I am not a huge Milton Bradley fan, but he's done for us what he has done throughout his career.  I would hate to see the Cubs eat a contract and I would like to see him back next year.   I don't really understand those that are critical of his overall performance.  I don't think he's imagining the heckling.  I've read enough anti-Bradley stuff to know that he's hearing it from the fans at Wrigley.  I don't doubt that he's hearing it around town, too.  Cubs fans have a right to be frustrated with the team this year, but I feel the anger towards Milton Bradley is misdirected.  I like his OBP and although he doesn't handle himself in the most rational way, he shows fire and effort every day.  I'll take that over the loafer in left field.
