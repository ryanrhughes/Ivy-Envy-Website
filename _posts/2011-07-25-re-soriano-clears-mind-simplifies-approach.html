I hope none of you missed this article by Carrie Muskat that was posted a couple days ago on Cubs.com.  The article consists of Alfonso Soriano trying to explain his recent struggles at the plate.  If you haven't been keeping track, Soriano has hit sub-.200 in the month of July.  He had also gone without a homer for about a month until he ended his drought against the Astros.

Soriano's explanation for his gelid bat: "The way we lose, the way we play, sometimes I think about the team instead of thinking about me first . . . sometimes you try to control the whole situation and mentally I get tired."

What?  That sounds like bad-job-interview-speak to me.  At some point you were probably told that during an interview, you may get asked about your weaknesses, and you should try to turn your weakness into a strength.  Soriano is basically saying, "Sometimes I just care too much," or "I'm such a team player that sometimes my personal well-being suffers."  I don't buy it.  Dolores, will you show this man to the door?
